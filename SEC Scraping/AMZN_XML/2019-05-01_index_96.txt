<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">We believe the Board of Directors&rsquo; fiduciary duty of care extends to

</DIV>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0"><B>ARGUMENTS IN FAVOR OF THE RESOLUTION ON RISKS OF SALES OF FACIAL RECOGNITION
SOFTWARE </B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0"><B>1. Amazon is exposed to financial, reputational, regulatory, legal, and
human capital management risk due to its sales of Rekognition to government.</B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0"><B><I>&nbsp;</I></B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">Investors are concerned by sales of Rekognition to government in the United
States and globally because this highly controversial technology poses tremendous risks involving civil and human rights and government
surveillance.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">Rekognition is a facial recognition technology tool that is being deployed
for facial recognition and recognition of other imagery in photographic and video recordings as one application available through
Amazon Web Services (AWS). AWS offers cloud-based products including &ldquo;compute, storage, databases, analytics, networking,
mobile, developer tools, management tools, IoT, security and enterprise applications.&rdquo;<SUP>1</SUP>
As promoted on the AWS web site, Rekognition is marketed in a suite of apps; it is offered &ldquo;free&rdquo; to new AWS subscribers.<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>2</SUP></FONT>
AWS&rsquo;s apparent intention is to offer apps that will induce subscribers to use more of AWS&rsquo;s cloud services.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0"><FONT STYLE="font-family: Times New Roman"><SUP>2</SUP></FONT> <FONT STYLE="color: #1155CC">https://aws.amazon.com/rekognition/pricing/</FONT></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">AWS is by far the largest provider of Internet &ldquo;cloud&rdquo; services
in the world, with 2018 revenue of $25.6 billion.<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>3</SUP></FONT> AWS currently provides cloud
services for all 17 United States intelligence agencies, as well as for government agencies internationally<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>4</SUP></FONT>
in the United Kingdom, Italy, Singapore, Belgium, Canada, and Turkey. The FBI is testing Amazon&rsquo;s Rekognition<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>5</SUP></FONT>,
and Amazon is attempting to sell Rekognition to Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE)<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>6</SUP></FONT>.
Florida&rsquo;s Orlando Police Department (OPD) and the city of Orlando, in July 2018, announced it would continue to use Rekognition
after an initial pilot program.<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>7</SUP></FONT> Amazon lists the Washington County Sheriff&rsquo;s
Office in Oregon, WA as a Rekognition customer.<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>8</SUP></FONT></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">According to the United Nations Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights
(Principle 17),<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>9</SUP></FONT> Amazon has a responsibility for the use of its products, and
human rights due diligence should cover &ldquo;impacts that the business enterprise may cause or contribute to through its own
activities, or which may be directly linked to its operations, products or services by its business relationships.&rdquo;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">In the hands of government, Rekognition threatens civil liberties and civil
rights for all members of society, and especially for people who are more likely to be surveilled, profiled and targeted, including
people of color and immigrants. Clare Garvie, of Georgetown Law&rsquo;s Center on Privacy &amp; Technology, has written<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>10</SUP></FONT>:
&ldquo;A mistake by a face-scanning surveillance system on a body camera could be lethal. An officer, alerted to a potential threat
to public safety or to himself, must, in an instant, decide whether to draw his weapon. A false alert places an innocent person
in those crosshairs.&rdquo;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">The American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU)<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>11</SUP></FONT>
has noted that facial recognition technology threatens to &ldquo;chill First Amendment-protected activity like engaging in protest
or practicing religion, and it can be used to subject immigrants to further abuse from the government.&rdquo;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">Amazon has repeatedly responded to the controversy in defense of Rekognition.
In February 2019, AWS&rsquo;s Global Public Policy VP published a blog post outlining key areas where the Company was taking the
unusual step of calling for enhanced regulatory policies surrounding facial recognition technology, especially when used by police.<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>12</SUP></FONT>
Amazon&rsquo;s blog post is a clear recognition that the Company has placed onto the market a technology that is most inadequately
controlled and regulated. Public expectations of privacy and respect for civil rights are not presently protected, and investors
need greater disclosure from the Company about how civil liberties, civil rights and human rights risks are being managed.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">While Amazon has been responsive to public controversy, the Company has been
less responsive to over a dozen shareholders who expressed concerns about Rekognition in June 2018. On June 15th, 2018, the lead
proponent of this resolution was also the lead signatory on an initial letter by 19 financial services firms holding Amazon stock,
including wealth management companies and registered investment advisors, raising strong objections to the introduction of the
Company&rsquo;s facial recognition technology. Not only did Amazon not reply to the initial communication, there was no acknowledgment
of receipt of the communication. Such initial issues raised specifically included &ldquo;substantial risks for our Company negatively
impacting our Company&rsquo;s stock valuation and increasing financial risk for shareholders.&rdquo; The June 15th, 2018 letter
also stated:</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0 0pt 0.5in">&ldquo;&hellip;we have seen no evidence of our Board of Directors
conducting fiduciary oversight on how Rekognition may or may not, should or should not, be deployed. The recent experience and
scrutiny of Facebook demonstrated the degree to which these new issues may undermine company value as the detrimental impacts on
society become clear. While Rekognition may be intended to enhance some law enforcement activity, we are deeply concerned it may
ultimately violate civil and human rights.&rdquo;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">While the accuracy of Rekognition&rsquo;s technology is a concern &mdash;
multiple studies by M.I.T. researchers and the ACLU have found that racial and gender bias is embedded in Rekognition<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>13</SUP></FONT>
&mdash; there are more fundamental issues, including whether facial recognition should be deployed at all because of the role the
technology plays in enabling ubiquitous government surveillance.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">Indeed, scientific and academic research suggests that over time the societal
risks embedded in a technology such as Rekognition may not decline but may instead increase quickly and dramatically as Rekognition
is deployed more widely. In April 2019, 25 prominent artificial-intelligence researchers &mdash; including experts at Amazon competitors
Google, Facebook and Microsoft, and a winner of the prestigious Turing Award &mdash; called on Amazon to stop selling Rekognition
to government.<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>14</SUP></FONT> &ldquo;There are no laws or required standards to ensure that
Rekognition is used in a manner that does not infringe on civil liberties,&rdquo; the AI researchers wrote.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">This is particularly concerning for those whose human rights are neglected
by autocratic government regimes. Human rights organizations cite the deployment of facial recognition in China, where, according
to one expert, &ldquo;surveillance technologies are giving the government a sense that it can finally achieve the level of control
over people&rsquo;s lives that it aspires to.&rdquo;<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>15</SUP></FONT></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">Scholars Woodrow Hartzog and Evan Selinger have written<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>16</SUP></FONT>:
&ldquo;We believe facial recognition technology is the most uniquely dangerous surveillance mechanism ever invented...when technologies
become so dangerous, and the harm-to-benefit ratio becomes so imbalanced, categorical bans are worth considering.&rdquo;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">Amazon is reported to be the second most trusted institution in the United
States<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>17</SUP></FONT>, and sales of Rekognition threaten this extraordinary relationship of
trust on which the Company relies for success. Given the serious potential risks outlined above, manifestation of these potential
harms could result in a negative financial impact on Amazon, through, for example, a decrease in sales or contracts due to diminished
consumer trust that negatively impacts the demand for Amazon products and services and increases the cost of business. Research
shows that being linked to adverse human rights impacts can negatively impact a company&rsquo;s reputation and harm its potential
to secure future contracts. A recent study quantifying the material impact of the reputational harm for Energy Transfer Partners
(ETP) due to its inadequate stakeholder consultation and public pressure from the Standing Rock Sioux Tribe to the Dakota Access
Pipeline demonstrated that social tension negatively impacted stock price and ETP underperformed relative to market expectations
as a result of the controversy. Growing and widespread social controversy and public pressure surrounding Rekognition may negatively
impact Amazon&rsquo;s financial performance.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0"><B><I>Privacy as a material financial risk </I></B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">Rekognition, as a potential tool for surveillance when sold to government,
already presents material issues for Amazon regarding consumer privacy. According to the Sustainability Accounting Standards Board
(SASB)<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>18</SUP></FONT>, which identifies environmental, social and governance factors most
likely to materially impact the financial condition or operating performance of companies in an industry, consumer privacy is likely
to be a material issue for companies operating in technology and communications. This includes the management of risks related
to the use of personally identifiable information (such as biometric data collected through facial recognition technology), social
issues that may arise from a company&rsquo;s collecting information (such as public controversies surrounding the collection of
facial imagery), and managing evolving regulation (such as regulation around the commercial use of facial recognition).</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0"><B><I>Reputational risk posed by sales of Rekognition to government </I></B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">The public controversy surrounding Rekognition presents critical risks to
the Company&rsquo;s reputation, including the willingness of consumers to trust the company to safeguard their privacy and civil
and human rights because storing your data on their cloud requires that you trust them to keep your data safe. Therefore, consumer
trust is one of Amazon&rsquo;s most important intangible assets. This comes as Amazon, with its leadership position in the technology
sector, confronts growing public criticism regarding the role of technology companies, including Amazon.com, and its products and
services, in societies and economies around the world. Thus, proponents believe Rekognition may threaten Amazon&rsquo;s long-term
prospects.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">Amazon is the subject of mounting public controversy because of this product.
In May 2018, the ACLU, along with a coalition of civil rights organizations, sent a public letter to the Company demanding that
it stop selling Rekognition to government.<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>19</SUP></FONT> In January 2019, over 85 activist
groups &ndash; including the ACLU, the National Lawyers Guild chapters, and Freedom of the Press Foundation &mdash; signed an open
letter expressing their concern for how Rekognition technology threatens community safety, privacy, and human rights.<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>20</SUP></FONT></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">Amazon&rsquo;s status as an employer of choice, and Amazon&rsquo;s ability
to be consistent with the values of its employees, is being undermined by the Company&rsquo;s internal management and potential

risks losing top new talent as millennials in the workforce seek employment from companies who can match their values. An Accenture
report found that 70% of 2016 college graduates prefer an employer who offers a positive social atmosphere, and companies that
&ldquo;do good&rdquo;, over a higher salary; 92% said it was important their employer demonstrate social responsibility.<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>33</SUP></FONT></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0 0pt 1in">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">In addition to products that present these concerns, surveillance issues raised
by Rekognition are mirrored within the Company itself in how it tracks its own employees. The Company was granted two patents recently
that would allow it to track its workers&rsquo; hand movements through wristbands. By tracking detailed movements through artificial
intelligence technologies, the Company can also obtain and record highly private information, such as when an employee takes a
bathroom break. This degree of monitoring adds Fourth Amendment privacy intrusion concerns to the mix of a work culture already
criticized for pressuring employees to work long hours and perform above all else, and investors need more information about how
surveillance issues may be threatening the long-term performance and health of the company overall.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0"><B>2. Investors need more information from the Company so they can be assured

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0"><B><I>Amazon&rsquo;s existing policy to mitigate risks of sales of Rekognition
are insufficient and ineffectual </I></B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">In its Opposition Statement, Amazon argued that the AWS Acceptable Use Policy
protects against risks posed by illegal or harmful use of Rekognition. Amazon asserts in the Opposition Statement that customers
who gain access to deploy Rekognition must comply with its Acceptable Use Policy which prohibits the use of its products &ldquo;for
any illegal, harmful, fraudulent, infringing or offensive use,&rdquo; including the violation of laws related to privacy, discrimination
and civil rights, and that Amazon has not received a single report of Rekognition being used in a harmful manner as posited in
the proposal. Recent experience, however, demonstrates that the Acceptable Use Policy is no guarantee that the AWS platform will
not be used in a harmful manner. According to multiple media reports<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>35</SUP></FONT>, a Mexico-based
news site used Amazon servers to openly store 540 million records on Facebook users, including identification numbers, comments,
reactions and account names. The problem was detected by researchers at an independent security firm which reportedly sent emails
to Amazon &ldquo;over many months&rdquo; to alert it to the problem. Amazon reportedly failed to respond to those messages.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">Amazon itself has shifted its stated position that existing company policies
protect users and the Company from significant risk. For example, in the face of the groundswell of concern by NGOs and civil liberties
experts, a leading company spokesman, as discussed above and in the Opposition Statement to the Proposal, tempered the Company&rsquo;s
position, calling for government regulation and &ldquo;dialogue&rdquo; among stakeholders, including shareholders. It is frankly
difficult to understand why the company is opposing the current Proposal, as the Proposal itself seems to provide the opportunity
to fulfill exactly the terms of Amazon&rsquo;s own invitation for &ldquo;open, honest, and earnest dialogue&rdquo; with key stakeholders.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">Shareholders are concerned with violations by government customers particularly.
Currently, the FBI is petitioning for face recognition systems to be exempt from the prohibitions on tracking people during the
exercise of their right to free speech.<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>36</SUP></FONT> In addition, long-standing rules that
have precluded the FBI and Department of Homeland Security from tracking the identity of individuals during the exercise of free
speech appear to be at risk.<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>37</SUP></FONT> Therefore, the Acceptable Use Policy provisions
are far from self-executing, and as the example of the FBI request for waiver of normal civil liberties protections demonstrates,
the evidence shows that the Acceptable Use Policy is ineffective at protecting people from the harms of surveillance technology
in the hands of government.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0"><B><I>Insufficient board oversight of risks related to Rekognition </I></B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0"><B><I>&nbsp;</I></B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">Governance of the significant risks presented by Rekognition is lacking. Instead
of company oversight, it appears that the responsibility falls to the customer for ensuring that the technology is not used in
illegal or harmful ways that threaten shareholder value. This is too significant of an issue for the company to abdicate its responsibility
to protect human rights. The Proponents are concerned that the Amazon board is not equipped to adequately identify and assess the
risks posed by Rekognition. The directors overall lack expertise that would give them the background or tools to assess the human
rights impacts of machine learning, artificial intelligence, and the primary technologies behind a product like Rekognition. One
possible exception is director Daniel Huttenlocher, who holds a Ph.D in Computer Science from MIT and hails an interest in &quot;emerging
technologies.&quot; The board also lacks any governance committee tasked with overseeing these risks. Therefore, an evaluation
conducted by an independent group of experts of the risks posed by sales of Rekognition to government is necessary to assure investors
that the Company is adequately assessing and mitigating risks posed by sales of Rekognition to government.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0"><B>3. Amazon lags peers in governance, oversight and management practices
to assess and address the ethical, legal, and reputational risks associated with the use of its products. </B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">Amazon&rsquo;s peer companies in the technology sector have implemented stronger
governance mechanisms to oversee human rights and ethics concerns posed by the sale of technologies. Amazon maintains three board
committees: a Leadership Development and Compensation Committee, an Audit Committee, and a Nominating and Corporate Governance
Committee. None of the committee charters make mention of human rights concerns or ethics in any manner. For a company as large
as Amazon with business interests with implications in as many areas of society as Amazon, this is an especially egregious omission
of purpose.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">Alphabet, parent of Google and competitor to Amazon in the technology field,
in December 2018 said it has opted to not yet offer facial recognition technology: &ldquo;...unlike some other companies, Google
Cloud has chosen not to offer general-purpose facial recognition APIs before working through important technology and policy questions.&rdquo;<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>38</SUP></FONT>
This is clearly a reference to Amazon, which faced extensive public criticism and reputational damage during this time period for
its pursuit of sales of Rekognition. Google dropped out of the bidding for a $10 billion Department of Defense cloud contract,
JEDI, due to factors including ethical concerns.<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>39</SUP></FONT></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">Brad Smith, president of Microsoft, called in June 2018 for government regulation
of facial recognition technology. At the same time, shareholders were publicizing a letter to Amazon asking the company to halt
sales of its facial recognition product and advocate researchers were making known errors in the quality of Amazon&rsquo;s product.
Google also spoke up at this time to announce a formal review structure to assess new projects, products and deals called Responsible
AI Practices, a set of quarterly-updated technical recommendations and results to share with the wider AI ecosystem.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">Similarly, in May 2018 Facebook announced an &ldquo;AI ethics team&rdquo;
in order to &ldquo;ensure that its artificial intelligence systems make decisions as ethically as possible, without biases.&quot;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">In the March of the same year, Microsoft formed the AI and Ethics in Engineering
and Research (AETHER) Committee bringing together senior leaders from across the company to focus on proactive formulation of internal
policies and how to respond to specific issues in a responsible way. Amazon has no similar effort. Microsoft&rsquo;s committee
however, set clear guidelines to identify, study and recommend policies, procedures, and best practices on questions, challenges,
and opportunities coming to the fore on influences of AI on people and society, and invest in strategies and tools for detecting
and addressing bias in AI systems and implementing new requirements established by the GDPR. Microsoft&rsquo;s cleaner reputation
in this area is a result of these measures.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman; margin: 0pt 0">Even without committees, companies demonstrate their commitment to establish
an ethical framework with new technologies by taking measures to call for their regulation, refusing to bid on or pursue contracts
with the government, or establishing specialty leadership roles. This month, Microsoft turned down a sale of facial recognition
software to a CA law enforcement agency, citing human rights concerns.<FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt"><SUP>40</SUP></FONT> Salesforce,
for example, employs a chief ethical and humane use officer. In all three of these proposed avenues, Amazon goes against the grain.</P>
