<P style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 8pt"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Dear Amazon Shareholder, </FONT></P>

<P style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 8pt"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>We urge you to vote FOR Proposal 11 at Amazon&#8217;s annual meeting on May 22, 2019. The proposal requests that Amazon&#8217;s Board of Directors prepare a public report as soon as practicable describing how Amazon is planning for disruptions posed by climate change, and how Amazon is reducing its company-wide dependence on fossil fuels. The report should be prepared at reasonable expense and may exclude confidential information.&nbsp; </FONT></P>

<P style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 8pt"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>We believe support for this proposal is warranted for the following reasons: </FONT></P>

<OL style="MARGIN-BOTTOM: 0in; MARGIN-TOP: 0in" type=1>

<LI style="MARGIN: 12pt 0in 0pt"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; LINE-HEIGHT: normal" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Amazon has already experienced numerous adverse events related to climate change, and faces increasing regulatory and reputational risks related to climate. Despite this, Amazon has released no information that allows investors to assess a) its plans for responding to climate disruptions and mitigating climate risk, b) its greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions and fossil fuel reliance, c) its goals and progress toward reducing that reliance. In other words, Amazon has not made it possible to assess how climate factors into Amazon&#8217;s strategy to create long-term growth or to mitigate risk.</FONT>

<LI style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; LINE-HEIGHT: normal" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Amazon lags its peers when it comes to climate change-related disclosure and action: while it has promised company-wide carbon footprint disclosure later this year, it has not made any comprehensive commitments. Existing commitments (such as Shipment Zero) are open-ended, not comprehensive, and lack context. Amazon&#8217;s peers have made disclosures and commitments on GHG emissions with far more speed and thoroughness.</FONT>

<LI style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; LINE-HEIGHT: normal" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Amazon has not empowered its internal leadership on environmental issues. Amazon's most senior sustainability officer operates four levels below from CEO Jeff Bezos. Comparatively, sustainability leaders at Microsoft, Apple, Walmart, and UPS are at no more than two levels below the highest leadership level. The sustainability leader at Apple reports directly to the CEO Tim Cook.</FONT>

<LI style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 12pt"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; LINE-HEIGHT: normal" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Over 6,700 Amazon employees (estimated ~1% of total workforce and ~10% of corporate employees) have publicly declared their support for this resolution. Leadership on climate offers Amazon an edge in recruiting high-skill employees; lagging on the issue makes Amazon a less attractive employer. </FONT></LI></OL>

<H1 style="PAGE-BREAK-AFTER: avoid; MARGIN: 12pt 0in 6pt"><B><FONT style="FONT-SIZE: 16pt; LINE-HEIGHT: 107%" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Rationale</FONT></B></H1>

<P style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 8pt"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Amazon is both affected by and contributes to climate change. Yet, despite numerous climate events affecting the company&#8217;s operations, clear risks to its reputation and brand, repeated requests to reveal its </FONT>

<FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions, risks to employee recruiting and retention, and systemic climate risks that Amazon&#8217;s business is exposed to, the company has still not disclosed its exposure nor risk mitigation plan. While Amazon&#8217;s Statement of Opposition lists many of Amazon&#8217;s &#8220;sustainability initiatives,&#8221; as evidence that it is already implementing this resolution, these initiatives do not constitute a company-wide plan nor clarify Amazon&#8217;s overall framework for approaching climate change. They do not address the disruptions caused by climate change. Most importantly, they do not give investors the means to understand how climate change factors into Amazon&#8217;s strategy to maintain long-term growth or to mitigate risk.</FONT></P></DIV>

<P style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 8pt"><B><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; LINE-HEIGHT: normal" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Amazon has failed to adequately disclose its climate related business risks</FONT></B></P>

<P style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; LINE-HEIGHT: 115%"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>A 2019 study published in </FONT><I><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Nature </FONT></I><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>indicates that the costs of climate change are in the trillions of dollars, but the climate-driven financial risk reported by all companies collectively was at least 2 orders of magnitude smaller.</FONT><SUP><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" face=arial,sans-serif>1</FONT></SUP><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" face=arial,sans-serif></FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Amazon is a prime example of a company that is not adequately disclosing or accounting for the risks to its business due to climate change. The following events are representative of climate change disruptions that have been reported publicly by the media without disclosure of the costs, mitigation efforts, or on-going risks from Amazon:</FONT></P>

<P style="MARGIN: 12pt 0in 0pt 0.5in; TEXT-INDENT: -0.25in"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=symbol>&#183;</FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 7pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face="times new roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>June 2016: An Amazon Web Services (AWS) data center in Sydney, Australia went down during severe weather, which broke rainfall records.</FONT></P>

<P style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt 0.5in; TEXT-INDENT: -0.25in"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=symbol>&#183;</FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 7pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face="times new roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>June 2017: Phoenix&#8217;s airport cancelled flights during a record-tying heat wave. At 120 degrees, airplanes struggle to take off and land. Disrupted flights are expected to occur in more cities serviced by Amazon Air.</FONT></P>

<P style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt 0.5in; TEXT-INDENT: -0.25in"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=symbol>&#183;</FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 7pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face="times new roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>March 2018: A data center supporting AWS suffered a power outage during Superstorm Riley, disrupting Amazon Alexa.</FONT></P>

<P style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt 0.5in; TEXT-INDENT: -0.25in"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=symbol>&#183;</FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 7pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face="times new roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>November 2018: A tornado in Baltimore smashed an Amazon fulfilment center, ruined its merchandise, and killed two workers.</FONT></P>

<P style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt 0.5in; TEXT-INDENT: -0.25in"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=symbol>&#183;</FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 7pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face="times new roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>November 2018: California&#8217;s Camp Fire temporarily shuttered Amazon&#8217;s Sacramento fulfilment center, delaying deliveries.</FONT></P>

<P style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 12pt 0.5in; TEXT-INDENT: -0.25in"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=symbol>&#183;</FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 7pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face="times new roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Feb 2019: Extreme cold temperatures (-40&#176; F) in Minnesota from a polar vortex shut down a fulfillment center for multiple days.</FONT></P>

<P style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 8pt"><B><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Amazon&#8217;s failure to provide ESG and sustainability reporting creates investor risk in an environment of increasing financial and regulatory risk around climate change &nbsp;</FONT></B></P>

<P style="LINE-HEIGHT: 115%"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=symbol>&#183;</FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 7pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face="times new roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>In April 2019, a group of 34 central banks around the world warned of the &#8220;strong risk that climate-related financial risks are not fully reflected in asset valuations.&#8221;</FONT><SUP><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>2</FONT></SUP><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Amazon&#8217;s lack of transparency about and planning for its climate risk means that investors cannot assess the company&#8217;s exposure to significant financial risk in the event of energy price volatility caused by energy demand or supply fluctuations or changes in government energy policy.</FONT><SUP><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>3</FONT></SUP><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Amazon could face financial risk in such scenarios due to the company&#8217;s over-reliance on fossil fuels, as evidenced by large investments in data centers in Virginia, a state still mostly powered by fossil fuels,</FONT><SUP><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>4</FONT></SUP><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>and Amazon&#8217;s recent purchase of 20,000 diesel vans for its delivery fleet.</FONT><SUP><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>5</FONT></SUP></P>

<P style="LINE-HEIGHT: 108%"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=symbol>&#183;</FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 7pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face="times new roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Without disclosure on GHG emissions, Amazon&#8217;s exposure to regulatory risk is largely unknown especially in an environment when rapid regulation around emissions is likely. Public support for action climate has increased globally (and in the United States), with a global median of 54% now believing it is a very serious problem.</FONT><SUP><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>6</FONT></SUP><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Given that 194 nations and the European Union have </FONT>

<FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>signed the 2015 Paris Climate Agreement (which </FONT><U><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=#1155cc face=arial,sans-serif>Amazon publicly supported</FONT></U><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>), the likelihood of significant legislation and taxation is rising in multiple Amazon markets.</FONT></P>

<P style="LINE-HEIGHT: 108%"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=symbol>&#183;</FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 7pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face="times new roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Despite Amazon&#8217;s pledge to power AWS on 100% renewable energy, the company has not wielded its considerable influence with utilities enough to achieve meaningful progress toward that end. In Virginia, likely the largest AWS hub, fossil fuels still power 88% of Amazon data center electricity.</FONT><SUP><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>7</FONT></SUP><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>The cost of utility-scale renewable energy generation, meanwhile, has fallen below the cost of coal and natural gas.</FONT><SUP><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>8</FONT></SUP></P>

<P style="LINE-HEIGHT: 108%"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=symbol>&#183;</FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 7pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face="times new roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Amazon&#8217;s sustainability website provides inadequate information for shareholder evaluation. As an example, the goal to install solar panels on 50 fulfillment centers (FCs) by 2020 comes without reference to total scale. What is not shared is that 50 fulfillment centers represent only ~6% of Amazon&#8217;s FC footprint. Similarly, Amazon provides data such as &#8220;reduced packaging by tens of thousands of boxes,&#8221; or &#8220;growing number of low-pollution electric&#8221; vans to demonstrate its commitments, but it does not provide any data to put these numbers in context, making it virtually impossible for shareholders to evaluate Amazon&#8217;s actions. Other plans come without a timeline, such as Amazon&#8217;s goal to achieve 100% renewables.</FONT></P>

<P style="LINE-HEIGHT: 108%"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=symbol>&#183;</FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 7pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face="times new roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Amazon&#8217;s Shipment Zero announcement, a goal to make 50% of all Amazon shipments to customers net zero in carbon emissions by 2030, does not commit to any net reduction in fossil fuel usage or GHG emissions. Given Amazon&#8217;s growth rate, greening 50% of shipments by 2030 will likely mean a net </FONT><I><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>increase</FONT></I><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>in the climate impact of Amazon&#8217;s shipping operations, relative to the current baseline. &nbsp;</FONT></P>

<P style="LINE-HEIGHT: 108%"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=symbol>&#183;</FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 7pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face="times new roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>As cities worldwide rollout regulations designed to limit urban fossil-fuel pollution, Amazon&#8217;s operations will continue to experience regulatory disruption. &nbsp;Congestion pricing has been enacted in multiple Amazon urban markets including London, New York, Milan, Singapore, and Stockholm, and they have already caused disruptions in last mile delivery.</FONT></P>

<P style="LINE-HEIGHT: 108%"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=symbol>&#183;</FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 7pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face="times new roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>The Sustainable Accounting Standards Board, which provides a framework for identifying material ESG issues, identifies energy consumed by infrastructure, data privacy, data security, product end-of-life management, managing systemic risks from technology disruptions, and competitive behavior and open internet as material ESG considerations. With $25.6 billion in revenue from AWS alone, this presents a materially large exposure for Amazon shareholders.</FONT></P>

<P style="LINE-HEIGHT: 108%"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=symbol>&#183;</FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 7pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face="times new roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>The company has repeatedly been asked to disclose its GHG emissions by investors, including Calvert (2011, 2015), Green Century, and Amalgamated Bank.</FONT></P>

<P style="LINE-HEIGHT: 108%"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=symbol>&#183;</FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 7pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face="times new roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Research by the Carbon Disclosure Project in 2014 shows that companies that lead on climate change management &#8212; including setting GHG goals &#8212; generate superior profitability, have lower volatility of earnings, achieve higher dividend growth, and exhibit other attributes that are valuable to investors.</FONT><SUP><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>9</FONT></SUP></P>

<P style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 8pt"><B><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Amazon&#8217;s failure to address climate risks may lead to reputational costs related to public perception and employee recruitment</FONT></B></P>

<P style="LINE-HEIGHT: 108%"><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=symbol>&#183;</FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 7pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face="times new roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </FONT><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>Inaction on climate is threatening Amazon&#8217;s ability to attract and retain scarce talent. </FONT><I><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>The New York Times</FONT></I><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>reported in Apr 2019 that thousands of Amazon employees (latest count: over 6,700 employees, which is an estimated ~1% of total workforce and ~10% of corporate employees) publicly signed a letter urging Amazon&#8217;s Board to adopt this resolution.</FONT><SUP><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>10</FONT></SUP><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>64% of Millennials report reluctance to work for a company whose corporate social responsibility record does not align with their values.</FONT><SUP><FONT lang=EN-US style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt" color=black face=arial,sans-serif>11</FONT></SUP></P>
