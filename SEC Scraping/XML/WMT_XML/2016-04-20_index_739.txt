<P STYLE="font: 14pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0 0 8pt; text-align: center"><B><I>Walmart Positions Board of Directors
for the Future</I></B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 12pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0; text-align: center"><B><I>Series of Changes at the 2016 Annual Shareholders&rsquo;
Meeting to Strengthen the Board<BR>
for Opportunities Ahead as Walmart Adapts to Evolving Customer Needs</I></B></P>



<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0; text-align: left; text-indent: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0; text-align: left; text-indent: 0"></P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0"><B>BENTONVILLE, Ark., April 20, 2016</B> &ndash; Wal<FONT STYLE="background-color: white">-Mart
Stores, Inc. (NYSE: WMT) today announced changes to its Board of Directors designed to strengthen the board for the future, while
maintaining its independence, diverse backgrounds of directors and breadth of experience. The changes include ordinary-course retirements
of four board members and a reduction in the size of the board </FONT>to maximize its effectiveness in supporting the company&rsquo;s
strategy. They <FONT STYLE="background-color: white">will take effect following the company's Annual Shareholders&rsquo; Meeting
on June 3, 2016 in Bud Walton Arena on the University of Arkansas campus in Fayetteville, Ark.</FONT></P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0"><FONT STYLE="background-color: white">&nbsp;</FONT></P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0"><FONT STYLE="background-color: white">Current board members </FONT>Aida
Alvarez, Roger Corbett, Mike Duke and Jim Walton, will not stand for re-election <FONT STYLE="background-color: white">at the company's
Annual Shareholders&rsquo; Meeting. The company also announced that as part of the board&rsquo;s succession planning, the board
has nominated Steuart Walton for election to the Walmart board. These changes will reduce the number of directors serving on the
board from 15 to 12 directors. </FONT></P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0"><FONT STYLE="background-color: white">&nbsp;</FONT></P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&ldquo;The contributions of each of our four retiring board members
cannot be overstated, and we wish them all continued success in the future,&rdquo; said Greg Penner, Chairman of the Board of Directors.
&ldquo;With these retirements, we view this as a time to make our board more nimble, while maintaining its independence and further
aligning <FONT STYLE="background-color: white">on Walmart&rsquo;s strategic priorities</FONT>. Our board has the right skills and
expertise to support the company&rsquo;s strategy. We continue to believe that the value, quality and diversity of our directors
are some of Walmart&rsquo;s greatest strategic assets.&rdquo;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0"><FONT STYLE="background-color: white">With the proposed changes,
the Walmart board will maintain its current independent majority at 67 percent. The board&rsquo;s 12 nominees, if elected, represent
an effective mix of deep company knowledge and fresh perspectives, including five independent board members added over the past
four years. Walmart has placed a focus on technology with the additions of Marissa Mayer, president and CEO of Yahoo! Inc., and
Kevin Systrom, CEO and co-founder of Instagram. In addition, Penner, appointed board chairman in 2015, provides extensive expertise
in technology, finance and international business. </FONT></P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0"><FONT STYLE="background-color: white">&nbsp;</FONT></P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">James I. Cash, Jr., Lead Independent Director, stated, &ldquo;The
changes we are making are designed to maximize our effectiveness as we adapt to ever-evolving customer requirements. We believe
that board refreshment and succession planning are critical and demonstrate good corporate governance practices, but also and most
importantly, support our mission to broaden and improve how we are serving customers through both stores and e-commerce.&rdquo;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0"><FONT STYLE="background-color: white"><B>About Aida Alvarez</B></FONT></P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0"><FONT STYLE="background-color: white">Alvarez is rotating off of
the board after a decade of service, consistent with Walmart&rsquo;s corporate governance guidelines. </FONT></P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0"><FONT STYLE="background-color: white">&nbsp;</FONT></P>

<P STYLE="margin: 0; font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif">Alvarez began serving on the board in 2006 and was a member of the Audit Committee, the Compensation, Nominating
and Governance Committee, as well as the Technology and eCommerce Committee during her tenure. She was Administrator of the U.S.
Small Business Administration and a member of President Clinton&rsquo;s Cabinet from 1997 to 2001. Alvarez was the founding director
of the Office of Federal Housing Enterprise Oversight, the financial regulator of Fannie Mae and Freddie</P>
