<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0; ">BENTONVILLE, Ark. April 23, 2014 &mdash;
Wal-Mart Stores, Inc. (NYSE: WMT) today filed its Proxy Statement in preparation for its upcoming Annual Shareholders&rsquo; Meeting
on Friday, June 6. The company also issued its 2014 Annual Report, its Global Responsibility Report and a special Global Compliance
Program Report on Fiscal Year 2014.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0; ">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&ldquo;Walmart has a rich history and is well positioned for the
future,&rdquo; commented Walmart President and Chief Executive Officer Doug McMillon in his letter to shareholders. &ldquo;Our
purpose remains clear &ndash; to save people money so they can live better &ndash; and the actions we&rsquo;re taking will expand
the opportunities to fulfill that purpose.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&ldquo;With greater convergence of digital and physical retail,
we&rsquo;re investing in capabilities to provide customers even more choice and convenience. When I think about all these capabilities,
I&rsquo;m confident in Walmart&rsquo;s continued growth and enthusiastic about our future,&rdquo; he said.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0; ">McMillon noted that Walmart is strategically
focused in areas that include:</P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&ldquo;Walmart&rsquo;s remarkable record as a retailer is based
on our unique ability to deliver on our purpose for customers, the strength of our culture and the foundation of strong governance
by our Board of Directors,&rdquo; said Walton. &ldquo;I&rsquo;m confident that Doug&rsquo;s leadership will provide Walmart a bright
and robust future, furthering the contributions of Mike Duke, who served exceptionally well as our CEO for the past five years
and continues as an advisor in the tradition of his predecessors.&rdquo;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 9pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0">In a separate release issued today, the company also announced
that Walmart Board member and former President and Chief Executive Officer Lee Scott and Board member Chris Williams will retire
from the board on June 6. Scott joined Walmart in 1979, and led the company from January 2000 until his retirement on Jan. 31,
2009. Williams has been a Board member the past 10 years and is rotating off the Board in accordance with Walmart&rsquo;s corporate
governance guidelines.</P>
