<div style="TEXT-ALIGN: center; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 18pt; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">NYC COMPTROLLER LIU, PENSION FUNDS TO </font><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 18pt; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">VOTE AGAINST NINE </font></div>

<div style="TEXT-ALIGN: center; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 18pt; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">WAL-MART DIRECTORS</font></div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="center"><font style="FONT-STYLE: italic; DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 12pt; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">Cite Persistent Compliance and Independence Problems</font></div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="center">
<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="center"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 18pt; FONT-WEIGHT: bold"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 18pt; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">******************************************<font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 18pt; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">***************************<font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 18pt; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">******************************</font></font></font></font></div>
</div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">NEW YORK, N.Y. <font style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: #ffffff; DISPLAY: inline">&#8211; City Comptroller John C. Liu today announced that the New York City Pension Funds will vote against nine of Wal-Mart&#8217;s 14 director nominees at the company&#8217;s annual shareowner meeting on June 7, 2013 in Fayetteville, Ark.&#160;&#160;The votes reflect the Funds&#8217; persistent concerns with the board&#8217;s poor oversight of compliance &#8212; with laws and regulations as well as the company&#8217;s own policies &#8212; and its overall lack of independence.</font></font></div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block"><br>
</div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 10pt"><font style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: #ffffff; DISPLAY: inline">&#8220;Wal-Mart has continually rejected stronger, more independent oversight &#8212; from its board room all the way down to its supply chain &#8212; to the detriment of shareowners,&#8221; Comptroller Liu said.&#160; &#8220;Concerns over Wal-Mart&#8217;s global compliance practices are rising, but the board today is even less independent than it was a year ago, when shareowners called for reform.&#160; Our Funds will continue to hold the responsible directors to account.&#8221;</font></font></div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block"><br>
</div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 10pt"><font style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: #ffffff; DISPLAY: inline">In 2012, following reports that Wal-Mart executives attempted to cover up alleged bribery in Mexico, the Funds opposed the election of five directors.&#160;&#160;That year, outside shareowners cast a resounding 31 percent to 38 percent of their votes against four of the directors, all of whom have been renominated for election in 2013.</font></font></div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block"><br>
</div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 10pt"><font style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: #ffffff; DISPLAY: inline">Since last year&#8217;s no confidence vote Wal-Mart&#8217;s board has become less independent, even as it has reportedly expanded its investigation into possible bribery to India. Meanwhile, Wal-Mart has fired garment suppliers after it emerged that they had violated Wal-Mart policy by subcontracting work to factories in Bangladesh&#160; at which a fire and a collapse have killed more than 1,200&#160; workers.</font></font></div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block"><br>
</div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 10pt"><font style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: #ffffff; DISPLAY: inline">As they did last year, the Funds will once again vote against CEO Michael T. Duke, former CEO H. Lee Scott Jr., Chairman S. Robson Walton, and Audit Committee Chair Christopher J. Williams.&#160;&#160;Last year&#8217;s press release announcing the Funds&#8217; opposition to these five directors is here: </font><font style="DISPLAY: inline; TEXT-DECORATION: underline">www.comptroller.nyc.gov/press/2012_releases/pr12-04-044.shtm</font>.</font></div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block"><br>
</div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 10pt"><font style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: #ffffff; DISPLAY: inline">The Funds will also vote their </font>5,148,594&#160;Wal-Mart shares <font style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: #ffffff; DISPLAY: inline">against audit committee members Aida M. Alvarez and James I. Cash Jr. for failing to ensure adequate internal compliance controls since 2006 and nominating committee members Douglas N. Daft, Steven S. Reinemund, and Linda S. Wolf for failing to ensure adequate board independence.</font></font></div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">Finally, the Funds will vote for all four shareowner proposals on the ballot, including proposals to require the board to name an independent chairman and to disclose whether it has clawed back pay from executives whose actions have caused significant financial harm to the company.</font></div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block"><br>
</div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-WEIGHT: bold"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; TEXT-DECORATION: underline">Background</font></font></div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block"><br>
</div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">Since 2005, the NYC Comptroller&#8217; Office and Pension Funds have raised concerns with the Wal-Mart board&#8217;s oversight of compliance, including with respect to various labor practices. Correspondence here: <font style="DISPLAY: inline; TEXT-DECORATION: underline">www.comptroller.nyc.gov/press/2012_releases/pr12-04-044.shtm</font>. In 2006, the funds filed a shareowner proposal calling on the company to implement the International Labor Organization and U.N. Human Rights norms in its international operations, including suppliers, and allow for independent monitoring of compliance.</font></div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block"><br>
</div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">In 2010, the funds filed a shareowner proposal to require the company to increase the transparency and accountability of its global supply chain; Kalpona Akter, a worker rights advocate and former child garment worker in Bangladesh, presented the proposal at Wal-Mart&#8217;s 2011 annual meeting in Fayetteville, Arkansas. In September 2012, the Pension Funds were appointed by the Delaware Chancery Court as one of three co-lead plaintiffs in a derivative action against Wal-Mart&#8217;s directors and officers relating to the Mexico bribery allegations.</font></div>
