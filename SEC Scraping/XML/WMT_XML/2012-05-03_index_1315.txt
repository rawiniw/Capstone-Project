<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">The recent <font style="FONT-STYLE: italic; DISPLAY: inline">New York Times</font> (4/22/12) report that Wal-Mart quashed an independent investigation into allegations that its executives systematically bribed Mexican officials from 2002 to 2005 suggests a disturbing disregard for legal and ethical compliance at the highest levels of the corporation.&#160;&#160;If the allegations are true, the company not only violated the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA), but may also have broken federal securities and other laws, possibly including criminal statutes.</font></div>

<div align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">Wal-Mart&#8217;s failure to pursue a timely, independent investigation in 2005 has potentially exposed the corporation and its shareowners to even more serious financial and reputational harm.</font></div>

<td>
<div align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">The audit committee failed to ensure adequate internal controls with respect to Wal-Mart&#8217;s legal and regulatory compliance, as evidenced by the company&#8217;s failure to respond appropriately when the bribery allegations surfaced in 2005. If the underlying bribery allegations are true, the company&#8217;s failure to detect the fraudulent accounting used to hide substantial illegal payments over several years would reflect weak internal controls over financial reporting as well.</font></div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">The audit committee&#8217;s failure with respect to legal and regulatory compliance is especially troubling given that, in three separate letters between May 2005 and May 2006, then-NYC Comptroller William C. Thompson, Jr., joined by other institutional shareowners, requested that Wal-Mart&#8217;s audit committee retain independent counsel to conduct a comprehensive review of Wal-Mart&#8217;s legal and regulatory controls and issue a report to shareowners with findings and recommendations.&#160;&#160;I encourage you to review the letters and the audit committee chair&#8217;s response, all of which we have now made available at <font style="DISPLAY: inline; COLOR: #0000ff; TEXT-DECORATION: underline">http://comptroller.nyc.gov/press/walmart.html</font>.</font></div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">The shareowners, who were unaware of the concurrent bribery allegations that have recently come to light, specifically expressed &#8220;serious concerns about reports of legal and regulatory non-compliance at Wal-Mart.&#8221;&#160;&#160;The Comptroller&#8217;s Office and allied shareowners discussed these concerns with two audit committee directors, including current audit committee chair Christopher J. Williams, in a meeting at the Comptroller&#8217;s Office on September 14, 2005.</font></div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block"><br>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">Following the meeting, the NYC Funds and other shareowners reiterated the request for an independent review in a letter dated November 30, 2005; the letter specifically cited &#8220;a cavalier attitude toward legal compliance at the highest levels of management&#8221; and included a three-page addendum with very specific questions regarding compliance systems, whistleblower policies and protections, and procedures for addressing allegations of wrongdoing, among other policies.&#160;&#160;Although unknown to shareowners at the time, only weeks earlier Wal-Mart executives had inexplicably rejected an independent investigation of alleged bribery of Mexican officials and instead launched a limited, internal inquiry, according to the <font style="FONT-STYLE: italic; DISPLAY: inline">New York Times</font>.</font></div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block"><br>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">Conclusion</font></div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">For the reasons stated in this letter, I urge you to vote against the five Wal-Mart directors named above.&#160;&#160;At present, the NYC Funds lack sufficient information to cast an informed vote on five additional nominees who have been on the board since at least 2005. Unless information becomes available clarifying whether or not these additional directors had knowledge of the bribery allegations and aborted independent investigation, the Funds intend to abstain on their election.</font></div>
