<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">On&nbsp;June&nbsp;25, 2015, the Board of Directors (the &#147;Board&#148;) of The Kroger Co. (the &#147;Company&#148;) declared a 2-for-1 stock split of the common shares of the Company (&#147;Common Shares&#148;) in the form of a stock dividend, for distribution on July&nbsp;13, 2015 to shareholders of record holding Common Shares at the close of business on July&nbsp;6, 2015 (the &#147;Dividend&#148;). Pursuant to the Dividend, each shareholder of record will be entitled to receive one additional Common Share for each outstanding Common Share held at the close of business on the record date. The Board also authorized the Company to make certain related proportional adjustments to account for the effects thereof.</font></p>

<p align="right" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:right;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Exhibit 99.1</font></b></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Kroger Raises Quarterly Dividend 13.5 Percent</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><i><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-style:italic;">Board Approves 2-For-1 Stock Split and $500 Million Share Repurchase Program</font></i></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">CINCINNATI, June&nbsp;25, 2015 &#151; The Kroger Co. (NYSE: KR) today announced that its Board of Directors approved a 13.5 percent increase to the company&#146;s quarterly dividend, a two-for-one split of its common shares, and a new $500 million share repurchase program.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;Today&#146;s actions reflect our Board of Directors&#146; confidence in Kroger&#146;s long-term performance and ability to deliver growth consistently to our investors,&#148; said Rodney McMullen, Kroger&#146;s chairman and CEO. &#147;The stock split will increase the accessibility of our shares and liquidity in the trading of our shares. We are especially excited that the stock split will make Kroger&#146;s common shares more accessible to all of our associates.&#148;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">13.5% Quarterly Dividend Increase</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Kroger&#146;s Board raised the quarterly dividend to 21&#162; per share on a pre-split basis. Because the dividend will be paid after the stock split is effective, shareholders of record as of the close of business on August&nbsp;14, 2015 will be paid 10.5 cents per split-adjusted share on September&nbsp;1, 2015.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Kroger has delivered double-digit compound growth in its dividend since it was reinstated in 2006. The company continues to expect an increasing dividend over time.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">2-for-1 Stock Split</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Kroger&#146;s Board approved a two-for-one split of its common shares. On or about July&nbsp;13, 2015, each shareholder of record as of the close of business on July&nbsp;6, 2015 will receive a dividend of one common share for each common share held. Based on the net number of common shares outstanding as of May&nbsp;23, 2015, the stock split will increase the net number of common shares outstanding from approximately 481 million to 962 million.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">This is the fifth stock split in Kroger&#146;s history. The stock split previously in 1979, 1986, 1997 and 1999.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">$500 Million Share Repurchase Program</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Kroger&#146;s Board approved a $500 million share repurchase program, replacing the prior authorization, which has been exhausted.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;Kroger&#146;s strong financial position has allowed the company to return approximately $11.7 billion to shareholders through share repurchases since January&nbsp;2000,&#148; said Mr.&nbsp;McMullen. &#147;We</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<div style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><hr size="3" width="100%" noshade align="left" style="color:#010101;"></div>

<div>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">remain committed to delivering value to shareholders.&#160; In fact, over the last four quarters, the company has returned more than $1.1 billion to shareholders through share buybacks and dividends combined.&#148;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">It is not expected that any purchases will be made under this new Board-authorized plan for the remainder of fiscal 2015. Actual purchases will depend on market conditions.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Kroger, one of the world&#146;s largest retailers, employs nearly 400,000 associates who serve customers in 2,626 supermarkets and multi-department stores in 34 states and the District of Columbia under two dozen local banner names including Kroger, City Market, Dillons, Food 4 Less, Fred Meyer, Fry&#146;s, Harris Teeter, Jay C, King Soopers, QFC, Ralphs and Smith&#146;s.&#160; The company also operates 780 convenience stores, 327 fine jewelry stores, 1,342 supermarket fuel centers and 37 food processing plants in the U.S.&#160; Recognized by Forbes as the most generous company in America, Kroger supports hunger relief, breast cancer awareness, the military and their families, and more than 30,000 schools and community organizations. Kroger contributes food and funds equal to 200 million meals a year through more than 100 Feeding America food bank partners. A leader in supplier diversity, Kroger is a proud member of the <i>Billion Dollar Roundtable</i> and the U.S. Hispanic Chamber&#146;s <i>Million Dollar Club</i>.</font></p>
