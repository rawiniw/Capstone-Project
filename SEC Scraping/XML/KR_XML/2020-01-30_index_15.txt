<p style="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt">&#160;</p>

<p style="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">In response to investor inquiries regarding the Lucky&#8217;s&#160;Market&#160;decision
to commence a voluntary proceeding in the Delaware bankruptcy court&#160;on Monday, January 27, 2019, Kroger issued the following
statement:</p>

<p style="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&#160;</p>

<p style="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">As Kroger disclosed in its third quarter earnings announcement,
as a result of a portfolio review, we made the decision to divest our interest in Lucky&#8217;s Market. Given this decision to
divest, we recognized an impairment charge of $238 million in the third quarter of 2019. The portion of this&#160;charge attributable
to Kroger&#160;was $131 million.&#160;The impairment charge was a non-cash charge and reflects the write-down of our initial investment&#160;in&#160;Lucky's
Market as well as additional funding provided to operate and grow the business. As a result of the Lucky&#8217;s bankruptcy&#160;proceedings,
we expect to deconsolidate Lucky&#8217;s from our consolidated financial statements which will result in a non-cash charge for
GAAP purposes in the fourth quarter of 2019. We do not expect any incremental impact on adjusted EPS&#160;guidance for fiscal years
2019 or &#160;2020 as a result of the bankruptcy proceeding.</p>
