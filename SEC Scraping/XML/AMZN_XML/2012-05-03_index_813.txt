letter of April&nbsp;18, 2012, regarding our Annual Report on Form 10-K for the year ended December&nbsp;31, 2011 (the &#147;2011 10-K&#148;). Each of your comments is set forth below, followed by our response. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><U>Item&nbsp;1A. Risk Factors, page 5 </U></FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><U>We Could be Harmed by Data Loss or Other Security Breaches, page 10 </U></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">1. We note your response to comment one in our letter dated March&nbsp;12, 2012. In order to place the risks described in this risk factor in appropriate context, in future filings please expand this risk
factor to disclose that you have experienced cyber attacks and breaches. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><U>Response </U></FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">We continue to believe that the cyber attack experienced by Zappos is not covered by the Division of Corporate Finance&#146;s Disclosure Guidance Topic
No.&nbsp;2 (Cybersecurity), which provides examples of risk factor disclosure in the context of a registrant that has experienced a material cyber attack. However, in light of the Staff&#146;s comment, we will revise our disclosure in the risk
factor captioned &#147;We Could Be Harmed by Data Loss or Other Security Breaches&#148; to read as set forth in our response to No.&nbsp;2 below. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">2. We note your response to comment two in our letter dated March&nbsp;12, 2012. In future filings please expand your risk factor disclosure to describe how the third-party technology and systems you
describe in your response fit into your business operations and confirm that the risks presented by your reliance on the third-party technology and systems are not distinct from the risks described in the first sentence of your risk factor or revise
your risk factor to disclose any distinct risks. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><U>Response </U></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">In future filings, we will revise our disclosure in the risk factor captioned &#147;We Could Be Harmed by Data Loss or Other Security Breaches&#148; to read as follows: </FONT></P>
 <p STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT SIZE="1">&nbsp;</FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px" ALIGN="center"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">1 </FONT></P>


<p Style='page-break-before:always'>
<HR  SIZE="3" style="COLOR:#999999" WIDTH="100%" ALIGN="CENTER">

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">&#147;As a result of our services being web-based and the fact that we process, store and transmit large
amounts of data, including personal information, for our customers, failure to prevent or mitigate data loss or other security breaches<U>, including breaches of our vendors&#146; technology and systems,</U> could expose us or our customers to a
risk of loss or misuse of such information, adversely affect our operating results, result in litigation or potential liability for us and otherwise harm our business. <STRIKE>In addition, we rely on third party technology and systems in certain
aspects of our businesses, including for encryption and authentication technology to securely transmit confidential information.</STRIKE> <U>We use third party technology and systems for a variety of reasons, including, without limitation,
encryption and authentication technology, employee email, content delivery to customers, back-office support and other functions. Some subsidiaries had past security breaches, and, although they did not have a material adverse effect on our
operating results, there can be no assurance of a similar result in the future. </U>Although we have developed systems and processes that are designed to protect customer information and prevent data loss and other security breaches, including
systems and processes designed to reduce the impact of a security breach at a third party vendor, such measures cannot provide absolute security.&#148; </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><U>Item&nbsp;8. Financial Statements and Supplementary Data, page 34 </U></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><U>Consolidated
Statements of Operations, page 37 </U></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">3. We reviewed your response to comment six in our letter dated March&nbsp;12, 2012. Please tell us how
outbound shipping charges related to commissions and service fees earned from seller services are classified. Please also tell us your consideration of presenting these shipping charges as a separate cost of services line item. Refer to Rule
5-03(b)2 of Regulation S-X. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><U>Response </U></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Outbound shipping charges related to commissions and service fees earned from seller services are classified within cost of sales within the Statements of Operations.</FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">These outbound shipping charges are only incurred for the subset of our sellers who utilize our Fulfillment by Amazon program
(&#147;FBA&#148;).&nbsp;Under this program, sellers&#146; products are stored in Amazon fulfillment centers in order to take advantage of our fulfillment infrastructure and allow their products to be eligible for Amazon promotional activities,
including free shipping to Prime subscribers.&nbsp;These products are subject to the same inventory control, fulfillment and shipping processes as Amazon owned products; and for multiple item shipments are included in the same box as Amazon owned
inventory.&nbsp;Therefore, we do not distinguish these costs from the costs of our product sales.&nbsp;Additionally, service sales represent a range of services in addition to third party seller fees, commissions and related shipping fees, including
non-retail activities such as AWS, marketing and promotion activities, seller sites, and our co-branded credit card arrangements.</FONT></P>
