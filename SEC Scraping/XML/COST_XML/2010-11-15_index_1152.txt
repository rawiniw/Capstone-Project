Board of Directors of Costco Wholesale Corp. (the &#147;Company&#148;) approved the fiscal 2011 performance criteria pursuant to which bonuses may be paid to executive officers. Under these criteria, executive officers (other than Jeffrey H Brotman,
Chairman of the Board, and James D. Sinegal, Chief Executive Officer) are eligible to receive bonuses up to around 20% of salary based on the achievement of specified targets relating to pre-tax income and operational characteristics relevant to the
executive&#146;s area of responsibility. (See Exhibit 10.1) The amounts of the actual bonuses are determined by Mr.&nbsp;Sinegal, subject to further approval of the Committee. Mr.&nbsp;Sinegal&#146;s employment agreement for fiscal 2011 provides for
a bonus amount of up to $200,000 (representing an amount up to 57% of his base salary), at the discretion of the Board of Directors or the Committee. Mr.&nbsp;Brotman&#146;s bonus is at the discretion of the Board of Directors or the Committee.
</FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">On November&nbsp;13, the Committee established performance targets for grants of restricted stock units (&#147;RSUs&#148;)
under its Fifth Restated Stock 2002 Incentive Plan to the following officers in the following share amounts for fiscal 2011: Mr.&nbsp;Sinegal 25,000 shares; Mr.&nbsp;Brotman 50,000 shares; W. Craig Jelinek (President and Chief Operating Officer)
40,000 shares; and all other executive officers 25,000 shares. Each of these grants, which are intended to qualify as &#147;performance-based&#148; compensation under applicable tax law, is subject to specified performance criteria for fiscal 2011.
The performance targets may be satisfied by the Company&#146;s achieving specified percentage increases, relative to fiscal 2010, in either total sales or pre-tax income for fiscal 2011. If the Committee determines that either or both of these
targets have been met, then the RSUs will be granted following the end of the Company&#146;s fiscal year and will be subject to further vesting thereafter based on the executive&#146;s continued employment with the Company through October 2014, with
20% of the shares vesting in October of each year beginning in 2010. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Item&nbsp;9.01. Financial Statements and Exhibits </B></FONT></P>

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px" ALIGN="right"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Exhibit 10.1 </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px" ALIGN="center"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Costco Wholesale Corporation </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px" ALIGN="center"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Executive Bonus Plan </B></FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">For fiscal year 2011, executive officers (other than Jim Sinegal and Jeff Brotman) are, depending upon the individual, eligible for a bonus award of up
to approximately $100,000. The final bonus amount is determined by Jim Sinegal, subject to the approval of the Compensation Committee. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Eligibility for up to 50% of the bonus amount is determined by whether the Company has met its pre-tax income goal for the year, as indicated in the
Company&#146;s internal budget. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">The remaining 50% is determined by goals relevant to the executive officer&#146;s area of responsibility, as
well as a discretionary component not linked to any particular metric: </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">For those whose responsibilities are operational, the
goals relate to sales, controllable expenses, shrink, and pre-tax profit in their areas of responsibility. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">For those whose
responsibilities are primarily buying, the goals relate to sales, gross margin, shrink, and inventory turns in their areas of responsibility. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:4%"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">For those who responsibilities combine operational and buying functions, the goals relate to a combination of those described in the prior two paragraphs. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">For those whose responsibilities are staff functions, the goals relate to a combination of Company-wide operational and buying goals, in
addition to qualitative factors relevant to their areas of responsibility. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">To be eligible for the annual bonus, the individual must still be
employed by the Company at the time bonus checks are issued (November). </FONT></P>
