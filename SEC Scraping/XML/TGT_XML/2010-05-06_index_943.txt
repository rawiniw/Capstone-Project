CORPORATION REPORTS APRIL SALES RESULTS</font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;punctuation-wrap:simple;text-autospace:none;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;punctuation-wrap:simple;text-autospace:none;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">MINNEAPOLIS,
May&nbsp;6, 2010 -- Target Corporation (NYSE:TGT) today reported that its net
retail sales for the four weeks ended May&nbsp;1, 2010 were $4,288 million, a
decrease of 3.5 percent from $4,446 million for the four weeks ended May&nbsp;2,
2009. On this same basis, April&nbsp;comparable-store sales decreased 5.9
percent.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;punctuation-wrap:simple;text-autospace:none;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;punctuation-wrap:simple;text-autospace:none;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;April&nbsp;comparable-store
sales were somewhat below our expectation. Based on our combined March&nbsp;and
April&nbsp;results, we believe a greater-than-expected portion of sales that
otherwise would have occurred in April&nbsp;were pulled forward into March,&#148;
said Gregg Steinhafel, chairman, president and chief executive officer of
Target Corporation. &#147;However, April&nbsp;sales in our higher margin categories
remained particularly strong, and both of our business segments continued to
outperform their respective profit plans for the first quarter. As a result, we
now expect Target&#146;s first quarter EPS will meet or exceed the current First
Call median estimate of 86 cents.&#148; All earnings per share figures refer to
diluted earnings per share.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;punctuation-wrap:simple;text-autospace:none;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;punctuation-wrap:simple;text-autospace:none;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">For reference, Target&#146;s
3.0 percent comparable-store sales increase for the combined March/April&nbsp;2010
period was stronger than any monthly performance reported since April&nbsp;2008.</font></p>
