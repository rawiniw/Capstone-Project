As used in this report, consistent with the Conflict Minerals Rule, &#147;3TG&#148; means columbite-tantalite (coltan), cassiterite, gold, wolframite, and the derivatives tantalum, tin, and tungsten.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" color="#c00000" face="Arial" style="color:#C00000;font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">A.&#160; Applicability of the Conflict Minerals Rule&nbsp;to Target</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Target is a retailer selling both everyday essentials and fashionable, differentiated merchandise at discounted prices. Target does not manufacture any of the products it sells. A significant portion of Target&#146;s sales is from national brand merchandise. Approximately one-third of Target&#146;s fiscal 2017 sales related to owned and exclusive brand products. Target may be subject to the requirements of the Conflict Minerals Rule&nbsp;because of the degree of influence that it may exercise over the materials, parts, ingredients, or components of some of its owned and exclusive brand products that contain necessary 3TG.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">For 2017, for purposes of its compliance with the Conflict Minerals Rule, Target had potentially in-scope products in the owned and exclusive brand merchandise categories in the table below (the &#147;Surveyed Product Categories&#148;). As discussed herein, for 2017, Target&#146;s outreach included all of the vendors of products that were identified as possibly containing 3TG within the Surveyed Product Categories (the &#147;Surveyed Vendors&#148;), regardless of whether the products of a particular vendor were believed to contain necessary 3TG and regardless of whether Target may have contracted to manufacture those products.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">The merchandise categories listed above are intended to summarize the types of owned and exclusive brand products Target sells that might be within the scope of the Conflict Minerals Rule&nbsp;and, as a result, are different than the five product categories Target uses for purposes of its other periodic reports filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission that cover all of the products it sells.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">For 2017, Target was unable to determine the origin of at least a portion of the necessary 3TG contained in the products of Surveyed Vendors. In some instances, Target was able to determine that some Surveyed Vendors for 2017 did not source from the Democratic Republic of the Congo (the &#147;DRC&#148;) or an adjoining country. Target&#146;s findings about the source of necessary 3TG in products from Surveyed Vendors for 2017 were limited by the fact that some vendors did not respond to Target&#146;s survey and that most vendors did not provide information about the source of necessary 3TG on an individual product basis, instead providing aggregate information for all of their products.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Target does not do any direct business with any smelters or refiners of 3TG. As a &#147;downstream&#148; company (which means a company between the smelter or refiner and the consumer), Target must rely on its vendors to provide accurate, reliable information about 3TG in its supply chain. Target also believes that, in most cases, it is several or more vendor tiers removed from the smelters and refiners of 3TG used in its products. However, through the efforts described in this Conflict Minerals Report, including its support of multi-stakeholder initiatives, Target is helping to address some of those difficulties presented to downstream companies and to encourage responsible sourcing in its supply chain.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" color="#c00000" face="Arial" style="color:#C00000;font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">B.&#160; Target&#146;s Conflict Minerals Policy; Participation in Industry Initiatives</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Target supports the humanitarian goals of the Conflict Minerals Rule&nbsp;and recognizes the adverse impact from mining and trade of 3TG by armed groups in the DRC and its adjoining countries. Target seeks to neither finance nor benefit those armed groups directly or indirectly.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Target also recognizes that there are many smelters and refiners that source from the DRC and its adjoining countries whose activities do not finance or benefit armed groups and who are certified as &#147;Conformant&#148; (as defined in Annex A). In order to avoid the undue harm to local populations that a generalized embargo upon this region could perform, Target encourages its suppliers to identify Conformant smelters and refiners within the DRC and its adjoining countries and to source from them when commercially practicable.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Target&#146;s conflict minerals policy (the &#147;Conflict Minerals Policy&#148;) seeks to balance Target&#146;s objectives of ensuring that its business activities do not finance or benefit armed groups and providing support to Conformant smelters and refiners in the DRC and its adjoining countries. The Conflict Minerals Policy states that Target will not knowingly purchase or sell any owned or exclusive brand product that contains 3TG that finances armed conflict in the DRC or an adjoining country. The Conflict Minerals Policy also encourages Target&#146;s suppliers to source from smelters and refiners within the DRC and its adjoining countries that are certified as Conformant.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">The Conflict Minerals Policy also includes Target&#146;s expectation that each of the vendors with which it contracts to manufacture will:</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="font-size:10.0pt;margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">2.</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font face="Arial">Complete a conflict minerals reporting template, which is the standard form developed by the Responsible Minerals Initiative (the &#147;RMI,&#148; formerly known as the Conflict-Free Sourcing Initiative) for gathering information regarding 3TG usage and related sourcing procedures (the &#147;Reporting Template&#148;);</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Target was engaged in early efforts to further responsible sourcing of 3TG. Target was a founding Executive Committee Member of the Retail Industry Leaders Association Conflict Minerals Workgroup, a multi-year program that helped retailers navigate the issues, compliance requirements, and solutions through a combination of educational materials, benchmarking, implementation tools, and key partnerships.&#160; Target is also aligned with the efforts of Oxfam&#146;s and Earthworks&#146; &#147;No Dirty Gold&#148; campaign and worked with Business for Social Responsibility to develop training for vendors.&#160; Target continues to be engaged with multi-stakeholder initiatives that encourage responsible 3TG sourcing and is a member of and participates in the RMI.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" color="#c00000" face="Arial" style="color:#C00000;font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">C.&#160; Reasonable Country of Origin Inquiry Information</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">For 2017, Target conducted a &#147;reasonable country of origin inquiry&#148; within the meaning of the Conflict Minerals Rule. To the extent applicable, Target used the same processes and procedures for its reasonable country of origin inquiry as it used for its due diligence efforts (in particular Steps 1 and 2 of the OECD Due Diligence Guidance, which are discussed later in this Conflict Minerals Report).</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Target&#146;s outreach to Surveyed Vendors included inquiries of 513 owned and exclusive brand vendors of products that were identified as possibly containing 3TG within the Surveyed Product Categories, regardless of whether the products of a particular vendor were believed to contain necessary 3TG and regardless of whether Target contracted to manufacture those products. For 2017, Target received responses from 470, or approximately 92%, of the Surveyed Vendors.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">For 2017, the Surveyed Vendors that provided product level information identified to Target 36 unique smelters and refiners. Additional information on these smelters and refiners is contained in Annex A to this Conflict Minerals Report.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Pursuant to the Conflict Minerals Rule, Target conducted due diligence for 2017. These due diligence efforts are discussed below.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" color="#cc0000" face="Arial" style="color:#CC0000;font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">D.&#160; Due Diligence Framework Used</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Design Framework</font></b></p>
