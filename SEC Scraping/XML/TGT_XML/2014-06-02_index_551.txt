<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Section&nbsp;1 &#150; Conflict Minerals Disclosure</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Item 1.01 &#160;Conflict Minerals Disclosure and Report</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Overview</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">This Form&nbsp;SD is being filed by Target Corporation (&#147;Target&#148;) pursuant to Rule&nbsp;13p-1 under the Securities Exchange Act and Form&nbsp;SD (collectively, the &#147;Conflict Minerals Rule&#148;). As used in this Form&nbsp;SD, &#147;3TG&#148; means columbite-tantalite (coltan), cassiterite, gold, wolframite and the derivatives tantalum, tin, and tungsten.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><i><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-style:italic;">Target&#146;s Conflict Minerals Policy; Participation in Industry Initiatives</font></i></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Target supports the humanitarian goals of the Conflict Minerals Rule&nbsp;and recognizes the adverse impact from mining and trade of 3TG by armed groups in the Democratic Republic of the Congo (the &#147;DRC&#148;) and its adjoining countries. Furthermore, Target seeks neither, directly nor indirectly, to finance or benefit those armed groups. The terms &#147;armed group&#148; and &#147;adjoining country&#148; have the meanings contained in the Conflict Minerals Rule. To support Target&#146;s efforts in complying with the Conflict Minerals Rule, Target adopted a conflict minerals policy (the &#147;Conflict Minerals Policy&#148;). The Conflict Minerals Policy includes, but is not limited to, Target&#146;s expectation that each of the vendors with which it contracts to manufacture will:</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">1.</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Adopt a policy relating to 3TG sourcing that is consistent with Target&#146;s Conflict Minerals Policy and Annex II of the <i>OECD Due Diligence Guidance for Responsible Supply Chains of Minerals from Conflict-Affected and High-Risk Areas: Second Edition</i>;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">2.</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Implement a due diligence program in support of its 3TG sourcing policy;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">3.</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Complete the EICC-GeSI Conflict Minerals Reporting Template;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">4.</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Exercise due diligence in seeking upstream information to support the vendor&#146;s responses to the questions in the EICC-GeSI Conflict Minerals Reporting Template;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">5.</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Use smelters certified as being free of conflict minerals whenever sourcing 3TG mined in the DRC; and</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">6.</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Make available its due diligence in determining the source of its 3TG upon Target&#146;s request.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Target also has taken an active role in 3TG responsible sourcing initiatives by the retail industry. Target is a founding Executive Committee Member of the Retail Industry Leaders Association Conflict Minerals Program, a multi-year program that helps retailers navigate the issue, the compliance requirements, and solutions through a combination of educational materials, benchmarking, implementation tools, and key partnerships.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><i><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-style:italic;">Applicability of the Conflict Minerals Rule&nbsp;to Target</font></i></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Target is a retailer selling both everyday essentials and fashionable, differentiated merchandise at discounted prices through 1,916 stores&#151;1,789 in the United States and 127 in Canada&#151;and at Target.com. Target does not manufacture any of the products it sells. A significant portion of Target&#146;s sales is from national brand merchandise. Approximately one-third of Target&#146;s 2013 sales are related to owned and exclusive brand products. Target is subject to the requirements of the Conflict Minerals Rule&nbsp;because of the degree of influence that it exercises over the materials, parts, ingredients or components of some of its owned and exclusive brand products that contain necessary 3TG. However, for a portion of Target&#146;s owned and exclusive brand products, it exercises no or minimal influence over the materials, parts, ingredients or components of the products. Due to that minimal influence, the Conflict Minerals Rule&nbsp;does not require Target to include those products and the related vendors in its conflict minerals disclosures. However, as discussed in the Conflict Minerals Report included as Exhibit&nbsp;1.02, Target included in its compliance efforts all of its 2013 vendors of owned and exclusive brand products in selected merchandise categories.</font></p>
<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">2<a name="PB_2_210845_7056"></a></font></p>

<div style="font-family:Times New Roman;">
<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Target does not do any direct business with any smelters or refiners of 3TG, so it has limited influence over them. As a &#147;downstream&#148; company (which means a company between the smelter or refiner and the consumer), it must rely on its vendors to provide accurate, reliable information about 3TG in its supply chain and on industry efforts to influence smelters and refiners. Target also believes that, in most cases, it is several or more vendor tiers removed from the smelters and refiners of 3TG used in its products. In addition, most of the vendors for Target&#146;s owned and exclusive brand products are private companies, which are not otherwise required to comply with the Conflict Minerals Rule. However, through its leadership in the retail industry, and through the efforts described in this Form SD and the Conflict Minerals Report included as Exhibit&nbsp;1.02, Target is helping to address some of those difficulties presented to downstream companies and encourage responsible sourcing in its supply chain.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Conflict Minerals Report</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">As required by the Conflict Minerals Rule, a Conflict Minerals Report is provided for 2013 as an Exhibit&nbsp;to this Form&nbsp;SD and is available at <i>https://corporate.target.com/corporate-responsibility/responsible-sourcing/social-compliance/labor-and-human-rights </i>under the heading &#147;Conflict Minerals Policy.&#148; The information contained on Target&#146;s website is not incorporated by reference into this Form SD or its Conflict Minerals Report and should not be considered part of this Form SD or the Conflict Minerals Report.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">As indicated in the Conflict Minerals Report, for 2013, Target was unable to determine whether any necessary 3TG contained in its owned and exclusive brand products in the merchandise categories included in its conflict minerals due diligence directly or indirectly financed or benefitted armed groups in the DRC or an adjoining country or are from recycled or scrap sources.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt 1.0in;text-indent:-.5in;"><b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Step 1:</font></b><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Establish Strong Company Management Systems</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt 1.0in;text-indent:-.5in;"><b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Step 2:</font></b><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Identify and Assess Risk in the Supply Chain</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt 1.0in;text-indent:-.5in;"><b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Step 3:</font></b><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Design and Implement a Strategy to Respond to Identified Risks</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt 1.0in;text-indent:-.5in;"><b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Step 4:</font></b><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Carry out Independent Third-Party Audit of Supply Chain Due Diligence at Identified Points in the Supply Chain</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt 1.0in;text-indent:-.5in;"><b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Step 5:</font></b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;"> Report on Supply Chain Due Diligence</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt 1.0in;text-indent:-.5in;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Our implementation of the OECD Due Diligence Guidance for 2013 is discussed below.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" color="#cc0000" face="Arial" style="color:#CC0000;font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">3.&#160; Due Diligence Program Execution</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">In this section, we provide a non-exhaustive description of the due diligence measures we took for 2013 pursuant to our 3TG compliance program, our Conflict Minerals Policy, the Conflict Minerals Rule&nbsp;and the OECD Due Diligence Guidance. Some of the due diligence measures discussed below are not expressly required by the OECD Due Diligence Guidance.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Step 1.</font></b><b><font size="1" face="Arial" style="font-size:3.0pt;font-weight:bold;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font></b><b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Establish strong company management systems</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">We adopted a policy relating to the sourcing of 3TG (the &#147;Conflict Minerals Policy&#148;). The Conflict Minerals Policy, publicly available at <i>https://corporate.target.com/corporate-responsibility/responsible-sourcing/social-compliance/labor-and-human-rights</i>, is described in the Form&nbsp;SD that includes this Conflict Minerals Report as an exhibit.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">The leadership of Target Sourcing Services, which is the sourcing operation for Target&#146;s owned and exclusive brand products, was responsible for implementing the compliance program. Target Sourcing Services designated dedicated employees, referred to as &#147;team members,&#148; to supervise the program and received support from other team members for additional corporate functions in compliance, finance, internal audit, legal, risk assessment, and communications. The leadership of Target Sourcing Services received regular updates regarding our 3TG compliance program.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">All team members with a compliance function within Target Sourcing Services are given training on the Conflict Minerals Rule&nbsp;and our 3TG compliance program. Other team members within Target Sourcing Services are also informed about the Conflict Minerals Rules&nbsp;and our policy with respect to sourcing products that might contain 3TG. The training materials remain available for review by team members who have questions. Selected team members also participated in conferences, benchmarking and workshops relating to the Conflict Minerals Rule.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">We supplemented our internal compliance team with outside professionals. We engaged a third party, Source Intelligence (the &#147;Service Provider&#148;), to collect and aggregate data from vendors to complement our own internal management processes. We also retained specialist outside legal counsel to assist with our compliance efforts.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">We used the Conflict Minerals Reporting Template developed by the Electronic Industry Citizenship Coalition and the Global e-Sustainability Initiative (the &#147;Conflict Minerals Reporting Template&#148;) to obtain information from our vendors concerning the sources of the 3TG used by them, and other information concerning their compliance activities. In addition, we are a founding Executive Committee Member of the Retail Industry Leaders Association Conflict Minerals Program, a multi-year program that helps retailers navigate the issue, the compliance requirements, and solutions through a combination of educational materials, benchmarking, implementation tools, and key partnerships. As part of the Retail Industry Leaders Association Conflict Minerals Program, we developed common approaches for determining the source and chain of custody of 3TG, and worked with Business for Social Responsibility to develop training for our vendors.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">We communicated our sourcing expectations relating to 3TG to vendors through direct communications, information posted on our vendor website, and online training. Additionally, vendors were provided with an email address at Target where they could direct any questions about our 3TG compliance program or Conflict Minerals Policy. As part of our engagement efforts, we communicated to all owned and exclusive brand vendors that Target would no longer accept any products that contained 3TG from the DRC or an adjoining country unless the 3TG could be verified as coming from a conflict free smelter. <p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">The Service Provider, on our behalf, sent communications to our vendors of owned and exclusive brand products in the included merchandise categories concerning the requirements of the Conflict Minerals Rule&nbsp;and our 3TG compliance program, including instructions on how to complete the Conflict Minerals Reporting Template. Through the Service Provider, vendors also were offered access to a vendor education portal and email addresses and telephone numbers for questions and guidance for responding to our information requests.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">In addition, all vendors and factories that produce owned and exclusive brand products for us are required to participate in our broader social compliance program. Additionally, every facility and vendor where owned and exclusive brand production takes place must authorize unannounced compliance audits. Through our broader social compliance program, we have the right, if deemed necessary, to conduct unannounced spot-checks of vendors who may have produced products that contain 3TG and have access to their documentation.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Step 2.</font></b><b><font size="1" face="Arial" style="font-size:3.0pt;font-weight:bold;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font></b><b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Identify and Assess Risks in the Supply Chain</font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">In order to obtain a broader view of 3TG in our supply chain, we decided to send the Conflict Minerals Reporting Template to each of our owned and exclusive brand vendors in any merchandise category with any products that we determined might contain 3TG, regardless of whether the products of that particular vendor were believed to contain 3TG and regardless of whether we contracted to manufacture those products. We took this approach because we believed that, for 2013, very few vendors, if any, would have the ability to report on 3TG usage at an individual product level. We identified merchandise categories with products that might contain 3TG through a process involving our internal product development team and input from our Service Provider based on its experience with other retailers and other industries. We did not filter out vendors or products in the included merchandise categories prior to sending the Conflict Minerals Reporting Templates. For a list of merchandise categories we determined might include some products that were covered by the Conflict Minerals Rule, see &#147;Section&nbsp;1.&#160; Product Status Summary.&#148;</font></p>

If a vendor continued to be non-responsive, the Service Provider was required to notify us. Our personnel followed up with the non-responsive vendors requesting the vendor&#146;s participation.</font></p>

Follow-up inquiries were made by the Service Provider to vendors to address other incomplete responses or where vendors indicated sources of their 3TG that were unknown to the Service Provider.</font></p>

We also believe that, in most cases, we are several or more vendor tiers removed from the smelters and refiners of 3TG used in our products. <p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">All of the summarized smelter and refiner information was furnished to us by vendors based on all of their products as a whole, instead of being limited to the specific products supplied to us. As a result, we do not know whether all of the identified smelters and refiners were actually in our supply chain. In addition, even if those smelters and refiners were in our supply chain, not all of the 3TG processed by those smelters and refiners that may have been contained in our products was necessary to the functionality or production of products that we contracted to manufacture. <p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" color="#cc0000" face="Arial" style="color:#CC0000;font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">5.&#160; Steps Taken Since End of Period</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">We expect to take the following additional steps in 2014 to mitigate the risk that our necessary in-scope 3TG benefit armed groups:</font></p>
