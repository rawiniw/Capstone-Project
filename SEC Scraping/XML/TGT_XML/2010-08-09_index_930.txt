your letter, we reviewed the MD&amp;A disclosures in our most recent Form 10-K.
Your letter requested that we expand our discussion to include additional
analysis of certain metrics and to address whether we believe the factors that
we identified constitute a trend and whether we expect that trend to continue.
With respect to your comment about providing guidance to investors on the
likelihood that our results are indicative of future performance, we typically
provide such guidance in the Outlook section of our MD&amp;A, and as such, a response
to this comment is provided in our response to Comment No. 2 below.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:justify;"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:12.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:justify;"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:12.0pt;">We operate
large-format general merchandise and food discount stores across the United
States. Our stores offer a wide assortment of general merchandise and food
items. Given the geographic dispersion of our stores across the United States
and the breadth of our merchandise offerings, our results are highly dependent
upon the general economic conditions within the United States, but no single
factor or small group of factors drives our sales or gross margin results. For
this reason, our discussion and analysis of our sales and gross margin results
typically focuses on sales trends during the most recent period as compared
with the corresponding prior year period and the mix of sales between
discretionary and nondiscretionary products, both of which are primary drivers
of our gross margin performance. Additionally, we plan our business expenses in
light of our general sales outlook to maintain an appropriate level of selling,
general and administrative expenses in light of expected sales. Again, no
single factor or small group of factors determines our expense rate.&#160; </font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:justify;"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:12.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:justify;"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:12.0pt;">After
reviewing our disclosures, we continue to believe that they adequately describe
and analyze the factors driving the key metrics of our business. We reviewed
the disclosures describing each of the key metrics within our retail segment
(sales, gross margin rate, SG&amp;A expense rate, and depreciation expense
rate) and the disclosures describing our credit segment performance. We believe
that our disclosures are appropriate and adequate. For example, the key metrics
in sales are fully described in MD&amp;A, including a detailed table of the components
of comparable-store sales, an important retail industry metric. We&#146;ve identified
the two key components, number of transactions and average transaction amount,
and we further subdivided average transaction amount into units per transaction
and selling price per unit. </font></p>

nondiscretionary purchases, the transfer of sales from existing stores to new
stores, and market share gains from other retailers. While these factors
certainly affect our sales, the effect of each such factor cannot be measured
with reasonable precision. In addition, sales are comprised of approximately

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:justify;"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:12.0pt;">We did not
include the restricted stock award granted to Mr. Steinhafel in March 2010
(fiscal 2010) in the Summary Compensation Table reporting compensation earned
in fiscal 2009 because of the guidance set forth in SEC Release No.
33-9089.&#160; On pages 23-25 of SEC Release
No. 33-9089, the Commission noted that the final rules require disclosure of
awards <u>granted</u> &#147;during the year.&#148; In this release, the Commission
acknowledged comments requesting that the aggregate grant date fair value of
equity awards be included in the year for which the related services were
rendered.&#160; However, the Commission noted
that multiple subjective factors could influence equity awards granted after
the fiscal year end, and determined to adopt a more objective approach based on
reporting the award in the year when the grant date occurred.&#160; The Commission went on to suggest that companies
use CD&amp;A to explain the post-fiscal year equity awards and consider using a
supplemental table if necessary.&#160; </font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:justify;"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:justify;"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:12.0pt;">The restricted
stock award granted in March 2010, while related to payment of Mr. Steinhafel&#146;s
2009 annual incentive compensation, was not contemplated at the outset of
fiscal 2009, nor was it within Mr. Steinhafel&#146;s discretion to elect to receive
equity.&#160; Rather, the Compensation
Committee exercised its discretion, after fiscal 2009, to award a portion of
his payout in the form of restricted stock.&#160;
As a result, the situation is distinguishable from Regulation S-K
Compliance and Disclosure Interpretations 119.25 (which contemplates a
non-equity incentive plan award, as opposed to an equity award) and 217.11
(which predates Release No. 33-9089 and also does not appear to involve an
equity award).&#160; </font></p>

we understand to be the final position of the Commission, we did not include
the aggregate grant date fair value of the restricted stock award granted in
fiscal 2010 in Mr. Steinhafel&#146;s compensation for fiscal 2009 as reported in our
Summary Compensation Table.&#160; However, to
explain that the grant related to services performed in fiscal 2009, we
included a footnote setting forth the aggregate grant date fair value of the
award so that a reader could calculate the amount of total compensation
including this award if they so chose, and also used a supplemental table in
our CD&amp;A to highlight this timing difference. </font></p>
