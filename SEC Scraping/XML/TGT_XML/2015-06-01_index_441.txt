<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Filed June&nbsp;1, 2015</font></b></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" color="#cc0000" face="Arial" style="color:#CC0000;font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Introduction</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">This Conflict Minerals Report is being filed by Target Corporation (&#147;Target&#148;) pursuant to Rule&nbsp;13p-1 under the Securities Exchange Act and Form&nbsp;SD (collectively, the &#147;Conflict Minerals Rule&#148;). As used in this report, &#147;3TG&#148; means columbite-tantalite (coltan), cassiterite, gold, wolframite and the derivatives tantalum, tin, and tungsten.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" color="#cc0000" face="Arial" style="color:#CC0000;font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">A.&#160; Applicability of the Conflict Minerals Rule&nbsp;to Target</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Target is a retailer selling both everyday essentials and fashionable, differentiated merchandise at discounted prices. Target does not manufacture any of the products it sells. A significant portion of Target&#146;s sales is from national brand merchandise. Approximately one-third of Target&#146;s fiscal 2014 sales related to owned and exclusive brand products. Target may be subject to the requirements of the Conflict Minerals Rule&nbsp;because of the degree of influence that it may exercise over the materials, parts, ingredients or components of some of its owned and exclusive brand products that contain necessary 3TG.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">For 2014, for purposes of its compliance with the Conflict Minerals Rule, Target had potentially in-scope products in the owned and exclusive brand merchandise categories in the table below (the &#147;Surveyed Product Categories&#148;). As discussed herein, for 2014, Target&#146;s outreach included all of the vendors of products that were identified as possibly containing 3TG within the Surveyed Product Categories (the &#147;Surveyed Vendors&#148;), regardless of whether the products of a particular vendor were believed to contain necessary 3TG and regardless of whether Target may have contracted to manufacture those products.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">The merchandise categories listed above are intended to summarize the types of owned and exclusive brand products Target sells that might be covered by its compliance with the Conflict Minerals Rule&nbsp;and, as a result, are different than the five product categories Target uses for purposes of its periodic reports filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission that cover all of the products it sells.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">For 2014, Target was unable to determine the origin of at least a portion of the necessary 3TG contained in the products of Surveyed Vendors. In some instances, Target was able to determine that some Surveyed Vendors for 2014 did not source from the Democratic Republic of the Congo (the &#147;DRC&#148;) or an adjoining country. Target&#146;s findings about the source of necessary 3TG in products from Surveyed Vendors for 2014 were limited by the fact that some vendors did not respond to Target&#146;s survey and that most vendors did not provide information about the source of necessary 3TG on an individual product basis.</font></p>

<br clear="all" style="page-break-before:always;">
<div style="font-family:Times New Roman;">
<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Target does not do any direct business with any smelters or refiners of 3TG, so it has limited influence over them. As a &#147;downstream&#148; company (which means a company between the smelter or refiner and the consumer), it must rely on its vendors to provide accurate, reliable information about 3TG in its supply chain and on industry efforts to influence smelters and refiners. Target also believes that, in most cases, it is several or more vendor tiers removed from the smelters and refiners of 3TG used in its products. In addition, many of the vendors for Target&#146;s owned and exclusive brand products are private companies, which are not otherwise subject to or required to comply with the Conflict Minerals Rule. However, through its leadership in the retail industry, and through the efforts described in this Conflict Minerals Report, Target is helping to address some of those difficulties presented to downstream companies and encourage responsible sourcing in its supply chain.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" color="#cc0000" face="Arial" style="color:#CC0000;font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">B.&#160; Target&#146;s Conflict Minerals Policy; Participation in Industry Initiatives</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Target supports the humanitarian goals of the Conflict Minerals Rule&nbsp;and recognizes the adverse impact from mining and trade of 3TG by armed groups in the DRC and its adjoining countries. Furthermore, Target seeks neither, directly nor indirectly, to finance or benefit those armed groups. To support Target&#146;s efforts in complying with the Conflict Minerals Rule, Target adopted a conflict minerals policy (the &#147;Conflict Minerals Policy&#148;). The Conflict Minerals Policy states that Target will not knowingly purchase or sell any product if it has reason to believe that a product contains any 3TG necessary to the production or functionality of the product that is used to finance armed conflict in the DRC or an adjoining country. The Conflict Minerals Policy also includes, but is not limited to, Target&#146;s expectation that each of the vendors with which it contracts to manufacture will:</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">1.</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Adopt a policy relating to 3TG sourcing that is consistent with Target&#146;s Conflict Minerals Policy and the<i> OECD Due Diligence Guidance for Responsible Supply Chains of Minerals from Conflict-Affected and High-Risk Areas: Second Edition </i>(the &#147;OECD Due Diligence Guidance&#148;);</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">2.</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Implement a due diligence program in support of Target&#146;s 3TG sourcing policy;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">3.</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Complete a conflict minerals reporting template, which is the standard form developed by the Conflict-Free Sourcing Initiative for gathering information regarding 3TG usage and related sourcing procedures (the &#147;Conflict Minerals Reporting Template&#148;);</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">4.</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Exercise due diligence in seeking upstream information to support the vendor&#146;s responses to the questions in the Conflict Minerals Reporting Template;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">5.</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Use smelters certified as being free of conflict whenever sourcing 3TG mined in the DRC or an adjoining country; and</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">6.</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Make available its due diligence in determining the source of its 3TG upon Target&#146;s request.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" color="#cc0000" face="Arial" style="color:#CC0000;font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">C.&#160; Reasonable Country of Origin Inquiry Information</font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">As required by the Conflict Minerals Rule, for 2014, Target conducted a &#147;reasonable country of origin inquiry.&#148; Target&#146;s outreach to Surveyed Vendors included inquiries of all owned and exclusive brand vendors of products that were identified as possibly containing 3TG within the Surveyed Product Categories, regardless of whether the products of a particular vendor were believed to contain necessary 3TG and regardless of whether Target contracted to manufacture those products.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">For 2014, the Surveyed Vendors identified to Target 48 smelters and refiners that processed or may have processed the necessary 3TG contained in their products. Based on Target&#146;s reasonable country of origin inquiry, it concluded that 33 out of these 48 smelters and refiners sourced exclusively from outside of the DRC and its adjoining countries, and 15 out of these 48 refiners and smelters sourced at least in part from the DRC and its adjoining countries, as more particularly described under &#147;Section&nbsp;F. Product Information; Identified Smelters and Refiners.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" color="#cc0000" face="Arial" style="color:#CC0000;font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">D.&#160; Due Diligence Framework Used</font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">The recognized due diligence framework that Target uses for its due diligence is the OECD Due Diligence Guidance. The OECD Due Diligence Guidance contains five due diligence steps:</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Step 1</font></b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">: Establish Strong Company Management Systems</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Step 2</font></b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">: Identify and Assess Risk in the Supply Chain</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Step 3</font></b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">: Design and Implement a Strategy to Respond to Identified Risks</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt 1.0in;text-indent:-.5in;"><b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Step 4</font></b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">: Carry Out Independent Third-Party Audit of Supply Chain Due Diligence at Identified Points in the Supply Chain</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Step 5</font></b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">: Report on Supply Chain Due Diligence</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">These elements of Target&#146;s program design are discussed below. <p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Target&#146;s Conflict Minerals Policy is publicly available at<i> https://corporate.target.com/corporate-responsibility/responsible-sourcing/social-compliance/labor-and-human-rights</i>. Target&#146;s existing integrity hotline has been designated as a mechanism for team members, vendors, and other interested parties to be able to report violations of the Conflict Minerals Policy. Mechanisms also exist for external stakeholders to contact Target by email, phone, or web form.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">The leadership of the Target Sourcing Services Quality and Compliance (&#147;TSSQC&#148;) team, which is the sourcing compliance operation for Target&#146;s owned and exclusive brand products, is responsible for managing Target&#146;s 3TG compliance program. TSSQC designates dedicated employees (referred to as &#147;team members&#148;) to supervise the program. TSSQC receives support from other Target team members in its legal, internal audit, corporate compliance and ethics, external financial reporting, and communications functions. <p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Training on the Conflict Minerals Rule&nbsp;and Target&#146;s 3TG compliance program is made available to TSSQC team members. Other team members are informed as needed about the Conflict Minerals Rule&nbsp;and Target&#146;s policy with respect to sourcing products that might contain 3TG. Selected team members also periodically participate in conferences, benchmarking and workshops relating to the Conflict Minerals Rule.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Target supplements its internal compliance team with outside professionals. Target also uses specialist outside legal counsel to assist with its compliance efforts.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

In addition, Target is a founding Executive Committee Member of the Retail Industry Leaders Association Conflict Minerals Program and continues to participate in its initiatives.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Target&#146;s policy requires the maintenance of its 3TG-related records for a minimum of six years. <p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Target communicates its sourcing expectations relating to 3TG to vendors through direct communications, information posted on Target&#146;s vendor website, and online training. 3TG training is made available to new vendors as part of Target&#146;s onboarding process and remains available to all vendors after onboarding. Additionally, vendors are provided with an email address at Target where they can direct any questions about Target&#146;s 3TG compliance program or Conflict Minerals Policy.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">The Service Provider, on Target&#146;s behalf, sends communications to Surveyed Vendors concerning the requirements of the Conflict Minerals Rule&nbsp;and Target&#146;s 3TG compliance program, including instructions on how to complete the Conflict Minerals Reporting Template. Through the Service Provider, Surveyed Vendors also are offered access to a vendor education portal and email addresses and telephone numbers for questions and guidance for responding to Target&#146;s information requests.</font></p>

If the identified smelter or refiner is not certified by one of these independent third parties, the Service Provider attempts to contact the smelter or refiner and/or consult publicly available information to attempt to determine the mine or location of origin of the necessary 3TG processed by the smelter or refiner.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Step 3.&#160; Design and Implement a Strategy to Respond to Identified Risks</font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Summaries of Surveyed Vendor responses are provided to the leadership of TSSQC. Purchases of the identified product will be suspended until the Surveyed Vendor can locate a source of 3TG that can either be verified as coming from outside the DRC or an adjoining country or from a compliant smelter or refiner in the DRC or an adjoining country. TSSQC will track any future violations of Target&#146;s policy by the Surveyed Vendor that supplied the identified product and stop doing business with the Surveyed Vendor if it is unwilling or unable to comply with Target&#146;s policy.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">6.</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">For those entities identified as a smelter or refiner by a Surveyed Vendor that were confirmed by the Service Provider to be a smelter or refiner, the Service Provider examined whether the smelter or refiner was listed as compliant by the CFSI, the LBMA or the RJC as of April&nbsp;30, 2015. Of the 15 smelters and refiners that were identified as sourcing at least in part from the DRC or an adjoining country, 14 were listed as &#147;compliant&#148; by the CFSI and one was listed as actively moving through the CFSI certification process. If the identified smelter or refiner was not certified by one of the foregoing independent third parties, the Service Provider attempted to contact the smelter or refiner and/or consulted publicly available information to attempt to determine the mine or location of origin of the necessary 3TG processed by the smelter or refiner.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">7.</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Summaries of Surveyed Vendor responses were provided to the leadership of TSSQC.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">8.</font><font size="1" face="Arial" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">In addition, to mitigate the risk that the necessary 3TG contained in its in-scope products directly or indirectly finance or benefit armed groups in the DRC or an adjoining country, Target made training on its Conflict Minerals Policy and compliance expectations available to all vendors, including new vendors.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" color="#cc0000" face="Arial" style="color:#CC0000;font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">F.&#160; Product Information; Identified Smelters and Refiners</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Due to the challenges of tracing in a multi-tier supply chain, Target was able to determine the origin of only a portion of the 3TG contained in its potentially in-scope products for 2014. For the list of Surveyed Product Categories that Target determined might include some in-scope products that are covered by the Conflict Minerals Rule&nbsp;as well as a description of the challenges of tracing in a multi-tier supply chain, see &#147;Section&nbsp;A. Applicability of the Conflict Minerals Rule&nbsp;to Target.&#148; Target received responses from 81% of the Surveyed Vendors.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">In connection with Target&#146;s reasonable country of origin inquiry or due diligence, as applicable, 48 unique smelters and refiners were identified by the Surveyed Vendors as potentially being part of the Surveyed Vendors&#146; supply chains. Of those 48 unique smelters and refiners:</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-21.7pt;"><font size="2" face="Symbol" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#183;</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">33 were indicated as not sourcing 3TG from the DRC and its adjoining countries;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-21.7pt;"><font size="2" face="Symbol" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#183;</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">14 were indicated as sourcing 3TG from the DRC and its adjoining countries, but certified as &#147;compliant&#148; by an independent third party; and</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-21.7pt;"><font size="2" face="Symbol" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#183;</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">1 was indicated as sourcing from the DRC and its adjoining countries, but described as actively moving through the CFSI certification process.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-21.7pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Arial" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Most of the described smelter and refiner information was furnished to Target by the Surveyed Vendors based on all of their products as a whole, instead of being limited to the specific products supplied to Target. As a result, Target does not know whether all of the identified smelters and refiners were actually in its supply chain. In addition, even if those smelters and refiners were in Target&#146;s supply chain, not all of the 3TG processed by those smelters and refiners that may have been contained in Target&#146;s products was necessary to the functionality or production of products that Target contracted to manufacture. Only products that Target manufactures or contracts to manufacture that contain 3TG that is necessary to the functionality or production of the products are subject to disclosure by Target under the Conflict Minerals Rule. The certification status of the compliant smelters and refiners is as of April&nbsp;30, 2015 and is based solely on the status as made </font></p>
