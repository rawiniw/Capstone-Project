an increase of 5.0 percent from $9,280 million for the five weeks ended January&nbsp;3,
2009. On this same basis, December&nbsp;comparable-store sales increased 1.8 percent.</font></p>

throughout the month drove sales growth in a broad array of merchandise
categories, including apparel, electronics, toys, food, and health and beauty,&#148;
said Gregg Steinhafel, chairman, president and chief executive officer of
Target Corporation. &#147;These sales results underscore our guests&#146; continued
confidence in Target&#146;s strategy and position us to generate
stronger-than-expected fourth quarter profit in our Retail Segment. Consistent
with prior guidance, we expect Credit Card segment profitability to be somewhat
lower than in recent quarters due to normal seasonality. In light of these
expectations, we believe that our fourth quarter earnings per share will meet
or exceed the current median First Call estimate of $1.11.&#148;</font></p>
