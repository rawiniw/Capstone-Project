<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0">Dear fellow Kroger shareholder:</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0; background-color: white">SumOfUs, an institutional shareholder of
Kroger, urges you to <B>vote FOR Proposal 8 at the annual meeting of Kroger shareholders on June 28, 2018.<SUP>1</SUP> Proposal
8 calls on Kroger to have an independent chair of the board of directors. </B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0">We believe a structure where the CEO runs
the business and is accountable to a board led by an independent chair is in the best interests of Kroger shareholders. Except
for brief &#8220;apprenticeship&#8221; periods at the outset of their CEO service, Kroger CEOs have also held the role of board
Chair for many decades. Kroger CEO W. Rodney McMullen is both the CEO and Board Chair.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0">We believe that independent oversight of management would improve
the ability of the Kroger board to support Kroger executives as they formulate appropriate responses to sustainability and competitive
challenges. As Intel&#8217;s former chair Andrew Grove stated, &#8220;The separation of the two jobs goes to the heart of the conception
of a corporation. Is a company a sandbox for the CEO, or is the CEO an employee? If he&#8217;s an employee, he needs a boss, and
that boss is the board. The chairman runs the board. How can the CEO be his own boss?&#8221;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0">An independent board chair is a corporate governance best practice
supported by the Council of Institutional Investors, GMI Ratings, Glass Lewis, and other corporate governance advisors. According
to Spencer Stuart, 51% of companies in the S&amp;P 500 separated the roles of CEO and board chair in 2017. CEOs that report to
a board run by an independent chair are paid about 20% less than their peers, according to a 2016 study by proxy advisory firm
Institutional Shareholder Services.<SUP>2 </SUP>We view CEO compensation as a visible indicator of the robustness of board oversight.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0">Investors look to the board to advance a long-term outlook, as a
counterweight to the shorter-term approaches of traders and company management.<SUP>3</SUP> Robust board oversight with a long-term
orientation is especially important as Kroger competes with retailers which have implemented sustainability policies that may be
more attractive to customers. A March 2018 study rated Kroger as &#8220;failing&#8221; on the category of pesticides in its supply
chain because it has no publicly available policy to reduce or eliminate hazardous pesticide use, while five of its leading competitors
rated higher in this category.<SUP>4</SUP> Fifty percent of Americans report being concerned about chemicals in their food, and
48 percent are concerned about pesticide residues.<SUP>5</SUP> Consumers cite avoiding pesticides and protecting the health of
themselves and their children as the top two reasons to purchase organic.<SUP>6</SUP> In a recent Natural Grocers survey, 90 percent
of organic consumers said they choose organic food &#8220;to avoid pesticides.&#8221;<SUP>7 </SUP>A 2016 poll found that 80 percent
of Americans believe it is important to eliminate bee-killing neonicotinoids (&#8220;neonics&#8221;) from agriculture.<SUP>8</SUP>
Kroger&#8217;s 87-page 2017 Sustainability Report does not make any mention of this issue.<SUP>9 </SUP></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0">Kroger faces both reputational and supply chain risks for failing
to address the overuse of agricultural pesticides such as neonicotinoids, glyphosate and chlorpyrifos.<SUP>10</SUP> A new report
by the Cambridge Conservation Initiative found that a dramatic decline in the number of pollinators such as bees and butterflies
could lead to many companies facing potential shortages of raw materials, a fall in crop quality and challenges around security
of supply because of an emerging pollination deficit.<SUP>11</SUP> One of the leading factors contributing to pollinator decline
is pesticide use.<SUP>12</SUP> A growing body of science has implicated the world&#8217;s most widely-used insecticides, neonicotinoids
(neonics), used on 140 crops, as a key factor in recent declines of pollinators.<SUP>13</SUP> The Task Force on Systemic Pesticides,
a global body of independent scientists analyzed thousands of studies and concluded that neonicotinoids represent a major worldwide
threat to biodiversity and ecosystems and called for an immediate stop to agricultural uses of systemic pesticides.<SUP>14</SUP></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0">Chlorpyrifos is a toxic nerve agent pesticide proven to cause health
impacts in people, including brain damage in children.<SUP>15</SUP> It has also been shown to harm pollinators and other wildlife.<SUP>16</SUP>
People come in contact with the chemical through residues on food.<SUP>17</SUP> Chlorpyrifos is so dangerous, that after years
of study the EPA planned to ban this pesticide. However, that decision was reversed by EPA Administrator Scott Pruitt.<SUP>18</SUP>
The science is clear that chlorpyrifos is unsafe and Kroger should stop selling food grown with this pesticide.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0">We believe that the issue of sustainability and brand damage related
to pesticide use is only one of many areas where independent oversight of Kroger management, by a board of directors chaired by
an independent director, would benefit the long term value of Kroger shares.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0">We urge you to vote FOR Proposal 8.</P>
