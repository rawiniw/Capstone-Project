<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">The Shareholder Proposal-Deforestation (Item No. 7) asks Kroger Co. (hereby referred to as &#8220;Kroger&#8221; or &#8220;the Company&#8221;) to improve transparency regarding its efforts to track and reduce the impacts its supply chains have on global deforestation and strengthen its corresponding policy to match that of its competitors. <div style="FONT-WEIGHT: bold">BACKGROUND</div>

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">Deforestation is primarily caused by cutting down forests to grow commodity crops like soybeans and palm oil, to raise cattle for the production of beef, and to supply the globe&#8217;s demand for pulp/paper. According to Chain Reactions Research, &#8220;as deforestation is largely driven by specific economic activities and is thus a sector-specific risk, investors and banks must now pay far greater attention.&#8221;<sup style="vertical-align: text-top; line-height: 1; font-size: smaller">1</sup> Kroger is among the world&#8217;s largest retailers by annual sales, and is, by way of its global supply chains, linked to the widespread deforestation associated with land conversion for commodity agriculture. Deforestation significantly degrades the environment in ways that directly impact agricultural production and pose a material risk to Kroger&#8217;s product offerings. Furthermore, public awareness and concerns about agriculture&#8217;s impacts on deforestation have prompted an unprecedented growth in demand for sustainably-sourced ingredients that do not contribute to deforestation. In response, major corporations, including key Kroger competitors Walmart, McDonald&#8217;s, and Nestl&#233; have announced and begun implementing policies to monitor and reduce deforestation across each of their commodity supply chains. Proponents are concerned that Kroger&#8217;s inadaquate policy efforts to address deforestation in its beef and soy supply chains, as well as its minimal reporting on the implementation progress of its palm oil and pulp and paper policies may expose the Company to financial risks.</div>

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">Scientific awareness and consumer concern about the environmental impacts of deforestation in the wake of unsustainable production within beef, soy, palm oil, and pulp/paper supply chains is rising, leading consumers to shift purchasing habits. Kroger lacks both the policy and metrics expected by consumers to address the perpetuation of deforestation, heightening its potential for reputational damage. If Kroger continues failing to effectively address and respond to shifts in consumer preferences in contrast to its peers, it could risk loss of market access.</div>

As of January 2017, Walmart Brazil has employed its Beef Risk Monitoring System to audit the sustainability practices of 100 percent of its beef suppliers on over 75,000 registered farms.<sup style="vertical-align: text-top; line-height: 1; font-size: smaller">8</sup></div>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
</div>

<table id="z4a2a15f8cd2b4d3ea18d444758f9006b" class="DSPFListTable" style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; WIDTH: 100%" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0">

<td style="WIDTH: 21pt"></td>
<td style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Symbol, serif; WIDTH: 18pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: top; align: right">&#183;</td>
<td style="WIDTH: auto; VERTICAL-ALIGN: top; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">
<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif">In April 2015, <font style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">McDonald&#8217;s </font>announced its Commitment on Forests, which outlined its strategy for ensuring the sustainability of its beef, soy, palm oil, and pulp/paper supply chains in order to meet its responsibility as a signatory of the New York Declaration on Forests<sup style="vertical-align: text-top; line-height: 1; font-size: smaller">9</sup> in achieving zero net deforestation by 2030. The Company releases annual sustainability reports and discloses progress on its commitments, such as purchasing a portion of its beef from a fully verified sustainable supply chain through a pilot program this past year.<sup style="vertical-align: text-top; line-height: 1; font-size: smaller">10</sup> Kroger has failed to become a signatory on the New York Declaration on Forests and scored 2 out of 5 in the Forest 500 2016 company scorecard, while McDonald&#8217;s received a 4 out 5.<sup style="vertical-align: text-top; line-height: 1; font-size: smaller">11</sup></div>

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif"><font style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">Nestl&#233; </font>is another signatory of the New York Declaration of Forests<sup style="vertical-align: text-top; line-height: 1; font-size: smaller">12</sup> which has committed itself to zero net deforestation by 2020. In 2013, the Company established and implemented its own publically available Responsible Sourcing Guidelines for its supply chains including those of beef, soy, palm oil and pulp/paper. By the end of 2016, 40 percent of the volume of 12 priority categories of raw materials set out by the Company had been assessed and certified as compliant with the guidelines.<sup style="vertical-align: text-top; line-height: 1; font-size: smaller">13</sup> Kroger has no such guidelines for its sourcing and lacks any policies regarding its beef and soy supply chains, an issue made apparent by the Company&#8217;s 0 out of 100 cattle scorecard.<sup style="vertical-align: text-top; line-height: 1; font-size: smaller">14</sup></div>

<td style="WIDTH: auto; VERTICAL-ALIGN: top; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">
<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">SUPPLY CHAIN RISKS</div>
</td>
</tr>

</div>

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">The consequences of deforestation threaten the environmental processes which sustain global food systems, resulting in risk for the global food system, the economy, and ecosystems. The vicious cycle by which unsustainable sourcing of beef, soy, palm oil, and pulp/paper causes impacts resulting in deforestation, which directly jeopardizes the production of those very commodities, could position the Company&#8217;s supply chains for future disruption.</div>

<div><br>
</div>

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; FONT-WEIGHT: bold; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">Deforestation alters and endangers crucial environmental systems supply chains depend on.</div>

<div style="TEXT-ALIGN: justify">
<table id="z8288dc815bcf4cabb5fcd758a082c403" class="DSPFListTable" style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; WIDTH: 100%" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0">
<tr>
<td style="WIDTH: 18pt"></td>
<td style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Symbol, serif; WIDTH: 18pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: top; align: right">&#183;</td>
<td style="WIDTH: auto; VERTICAL-ALIGN: top; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">
<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif">Tropical forests hold 210 gigatons of carbon, a greenhouse gas that significantly contributes to global warming, changes in weather patterns, disruption of the water cycle, and the frequency of extreme weather events, all of which threaten the security of commodity supply chains.<sup style="vertical-align: text-top; line-height: 1; font-size: smaller">15</sup></div>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
</div>

<div style="TEXT-ALIGN: justify">
<table id="zd6962f16eef74d30aff3752887a3f06a" class="DSPFListTable" style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; WIDTH: 100%" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0">
<tr>
<td style="WIDTH: 18pt"></td>
<td style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Symbol, serif; WIDTH: 18pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: top; align: right">&#183;</td>
<td style="WIDTH: auto; VERTICAL-ALIGN: top; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">
<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif">Deforestation alters global rainfall patterns, with some studies indicating that deforestation has led to 10-15 percent declines in local rainfall and shifts in rainfall patterns thousands of miles away.<sup style="vertical-align: text-top; line-height: 1; font-size: smaller">16</sup> This is because clearing of forests disrupts natural hydrological cycles that depend on trees to absorb, filter, and evaporate freshwater. Agriculture is highly dependent on consistent rainfall patterns, yet multiple studies have indicated that deforestation, due primarily to agricultural expansion, is leading to reductions in rainfall.<sup style="vertical-align: text-top; line-height: 1; font-size: smaller">17</sup></div>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
</div>

<div style="TEXT-ALIGN: justify">
<table id="zee77901443764d638553c485d45e95db" class="DSPFListTable" style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; WIDTH: 100%" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0">
<tr>
<td style="WIDTH: 18pt"></td>
<td style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Symbol, serif; WIDTH: 18pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: top; align: right">&#183;</td>
<td style="WIDTH: auto; VERTICAL-ALIGN: top; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">
<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif">According to a 2015 study, nearly 33 percent of the world&#8217;s adequate or high-quality food-producing land has already been lost to soil erosion caused by the lack of trees necessary for anchoring the fertile soil. As fertile soil disappears, producers clear more forest and thereby perpetuate a cycle of degradation, which estimates warn could damage the entirety of the world&#8217;s top soil within 60 years.<sup style="vertical-align: text-top; line-height: 1; font-size: smaller">18</sup></div>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
</div>

<table id="z038712729430464da13df0ae530b3eb4" class="DSPFListTable" style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; WIDTH: 100%" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0">

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif">A study carried out by CDP found that between cattle, palm oil, soy and timber product supply chains, approximately $906 billion in annual turnover is potentially at risk from the effects of deforestation.<sup style="vertical-align: text-top; line-height: 1; font-size: smaller">19</sup></div>
</td>

<div style="FONT-WEIGHT: bold">CONCLUSION</div>

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">Deforestation contributes to material risks in commodity production and therefore Kroger&#8217;s ability to provide products for its retail business. While Kroger has committed to policies that address deforestation in its global supply chains, their comparative weakness when considered with the Company&#8217;s industry peers and narrow scope does not adequately protect Kroger or its investors from the risks assoiated with deforestation. Given that commodity agriculture is the single leading driver of deforestation, Kroger&#8217;s commodity-based supply chains remain of concern. Similarly, as competitors implement stronger initiatives to tackle deforestation within their own supply chains and consumers continue to demonstrate demand for sustainably sourced products, the Company runs the risk of diminishing its reputation to <font style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; COLOR: #000000">that of a laggard as well as losing significant market access. As the environmental impacts of deforestation and the rising consumer concerns regarding the issue become more pronounced, proponents are concerned that Kroger faces significant business risks if it fails to more aggressively manage deforestation occurring throughout its supply chains.</font></div>
