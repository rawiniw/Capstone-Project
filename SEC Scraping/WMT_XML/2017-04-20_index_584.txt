<P STYLE="font: 12pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: center"><B>Walmart Releases 2017 Annual Report, Proxy
Statement, Global Responsibility Report and Global Ethics and Compliance Program Update </B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 12pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: center">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0; background-color: white"><B>BENTONVILLE, Ark., April 20, 2017</B>
&mdash; Wal-Mart Stores, Inc. (NYSE: WMT) today issued its 2017 Annual Report, Global Responsibility Report, an update on its Global
Ethics and Compliance Program and filed its Proxy Statement in preparation for the company&rsquo;s upcoming Annual Shareholders&rsquo;
Meeting on Friday, June 2, 2017.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0; background-color: white">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&ldquo;We are a company of the future. We&rsquo;re operating
from a strong foundation built by those before us and taking action aimed at strengthening our business this year and beyond,&rdquo;
Walmart President and Chief Executive Officer Doug McMillon said in his letter to shareholders, associates and customers. &ldquo;We&rsquo;ll
continue to strengthen our stores around the world, we&rsquo;ll continue to build our e-commerce and digital capabilities and we&rsquo;ll
put them together in a way that saves customers time and money.&rdquo;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0"><B>Annual Report Highlights </B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0">The 2017 Annual Report, Moving with Speed, provides shareholders
with highlights of Walmart&rsquo;s achievements in the last fiscal year and key company-wide initiatives for the upcoming year. The
full report is available <U>here</U>.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0">In the report, McMillon highlights how the company is
continuing to write its own history of innovation by harnessing the power of technology and its investments in associates to
create new ways of serving customers and providing associates with more opportunities to grow their careers. The company
plans to focus on four key objectives in the coming year - make every day easier for busy families, change the way Walmart
works, deliver results and operate with discipline and be the most trusted retailer - to take advantage of opportunities
resulting from the fundamental transformation of the retail industry within the economy and the communities Walmart
serves.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0">Also in the report is an update on governance and shareholder
engagement from Chairman of the Board of Directors, Greg Penner. In his letter to shareholders, Penner states, &ldquo;The transformation
of Walmart is gaining momentum, and the Board of Directors is confident that we have the right strategy to position the business
for sustainable growth and shareholder returns in this new era of retail. Today&rsquo;s customer has more time pressures than ever,
and our focus on making life easier for busy families is critical. Walmart is uniquely positioned to deliver on that goal.&rdquo;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0; background-color: white"><B>2017 Annual Shareholders&rsquo; Meeting
and Proxy Statement</B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0; background-color: white">The company began distributing its Proxy
Statement today with details of its Annual Shareholders&rsquo; Meeting, to be held on Friday, June 2, 2017, at 8 a.m. (CDT) in
Bud Walton Arena on the University of Arkansas campus in Fayetteville, Ark. Shareholders of record as of the close of business
on April 7, 2017, the record date for the meeting, are entitled to vote at this year&rsquo;s meeting.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0; background-color: white">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0">This year&rsquo;s Proxy materials seek Shareholders&rsquo; vote
on 11 director nominees, three company proposals and three shareholder proposals. Linda Wolf and Pam Craig are r<FONT STYLE="color: #222222; background-color: white">etiring
from the Board and&nbsp;</FONT>will not stand for re-election. As Walmart maintains its commitment to an independent board, it
announced Carla Harris, vice chair of the Wealth Management business segment of Morgan Stanley, as a new independent nominee.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0; background-color: white">&ldquo;Linda and Pam have provided insightful
leadership and demonstrated a deep commitment to the Board and shareholders that will be missed,&rdquo; Penner said. &ldquo;Linda&rsquo;s
brand management and international business expertise have benefited Walmart tremendously while Pam&rsquo;s global perspectives,
finance and</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0">Wolf, who served as chair of the Compensation and Management

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0; background-color: white">Craig joined the Board in 2013 and served
on the Technology and eCommerce Committee and the Audit Committee. She and Walmart mutually agreed that she will not stand for
re-election due to the technical meaning of &ldquo;independent&rdquo; under the NYSE&rsquo;s rules. As part of its annual review,
the Board recently determined that Craig is no longer independent based on the 2016 promotion of her brother-in-law to executive
officer of a Walmart supplier. Payments by Walmart to the supplier account for more than two percent of the supplier&rsquo;s annual
revenues If not for this technical issue, the Board otherwise considers Craig independent due to the fact that her brother-in-law
is responsible for a region with the supplier that does a minimal amount of business with Walmart. Neither have a direct nor indirect
material interest in the relationship between Walmart and the supplier.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0; background-color: white">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0pt 0">Harris&rsquo; experience during her 30-year career with Morgan
Stanley has included investment banking, equity capital markets, equity private placements, and initial public offerings in a number
of industries such as technology, media, retail, telecommunications, transportation, healthcare and biotechnology. In 2013, she
was also appointed by President Obama to serve as Chair of the National Women&rsquo;s Business Council.</P>
