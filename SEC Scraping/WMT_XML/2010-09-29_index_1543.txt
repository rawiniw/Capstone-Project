 <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Item&nbsp;5.02. Departure of Directors or Certain Officers; Election of Directors; Appointment of Certain
Officers; Compensatory Arrangements of Certain Officers. </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">(b) On September&nbsp;23, 2010, Thomas M. Schoewe, executive vice
president and chief financial officer of Wal-Mart Stores, Inc. (the &#147;Company&#148;) notified the Company that he will retire from his position as executive vice president and chief financial officer, effective on the close of business on
November&nbsp;30, 2010. On December&nbsp;1, 2010, Mr.&nbsp;Schoewe will continue in his capacity as an executive vice president and be employed as an associate of the Company through January&nbsp;31, 2011. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">(c) On September&nbsp;24, 2010, the Board of Directors appointed Charles M. Holley, age 54, as executive president and chief financial
officer of the Company, effective as of the close of business on November&nbsp;30, 2010. Since January 2007, Mr.&nbsp;Holley has served as the Company&#146;s executive vice president-finance and treasurer. From 2005 to 2007, Mr.&nbsp;Holley served
as a senior vice president of finance with responsibility for the Company&#146;s accounting, tax, financial reporting and investor relations areas, as well as financial support responsibility for the Company&#146;s real estate, benefits, risk
management and information systems areas. From 2003 to 2005, Mr.&nbsp;Holley served as the Company&#146;s senior vice president and controller. From 1999 to 2003, Mr.&nbsp;Holley was a senior vice president and chief financial officer of Walmart
International, which included, in addition to other duties, the responsibility for the Company&#146;s international acquisitions. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">The Company issued a press release on September&nbsp;29, 2010, announcing the appointment of Mr.&nbsp;Holley in his new role. A copy of
the press release is furnished as Exhibit 99.1 to this report. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">The Company entered into a post-termination agreement and
covenant not to compete with Mr.&nbsp;Holley on March&nbsp;14, 2010 (the &#147;Noncompete Agreement&#148;). The Noncompete Agreement is substantially similar to the form of post-termination agreement and covenant not to compete that is attached as
Exhibit 10(p) to the Company&#146;s Form 10-K filed on March&nbsp;30, 2010. The Noncompete Agreement prohibits Mr.&nbsp;Holley, for a period of two years following termination of employment with the Company for any reason, from participating in a
business that competes with the Company and from soliciting the Company&#146;s associates for employment. The Noncompete Agreement also provides that, if Mr.&nbsp;Holley&#146;s employment is terminated by the Company for any reason, other than for a
violation of the Company&#146;s policies, then the Company will continue to pay Mr.&nbsp;Holley&#146;s base salary for two years following termination of employment. As consideration for executing the Noncompete Agreement, Mr.&nbsp;Holley received a
grant of 17,886 shares of restricted stock. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">(e) On September&nbsp;28, 2010, the Company entered into a retirement agreement
with Mr.&nbsp;Schoewe, which provides that Mr.&nbsp;Schoewe&#146;s employment will continue through January&nbsp;31, 2011 (the &#147;Agreement&#148;). The Agreement is attached as Exhibit 10.1 and is incorporated herein by reference.
Mr.&nbsp;Schoewe will receive his salary through January&nbsp;31, 2011 and he will be eligible to participate in the Company&#146;s Management Incentive Plan and his unvested stock options, restricted stock and performance shares will continue to
vest in the normal course through January&nbsp;31, 2011, with several exceptions. Mr.&nbsp;Schoewe will forfeit 100 percent of the 69,804 target performance shares he received on January&nbsp;23, 2009, and 100 percent of the 62,465 target
performance shares he received on January&nbsp;21, 2010. The Company will accelerate certain restricted stock grants and stock option grants that are scheduled to vest in 2012, 2013, 2014 and 2015 as reflected in the Agreement. The Agreement, which
also operates as an amendment to Mr.&nbsp;Schoewe&#146;s covenant not to compete agreement, extends the non-competition and non-solicitation provisions through January&nbsp;31, 2014. </FONT></P>
