<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; FONT-WEIGHT: bold; TEXT-ALIGN: left; MARGIN-LEFT: 18pt; TEXT-INDENT: -18pt"><u>1. Pay equity is a significant issue in the US.</u></div>

<div style="TEXT-ALIGN: left; MARGIN-LEFT: 18pt; TEXT-INDENT: -18pt"><br>
</div>

<div style="TEXT-ALIGN: left">
<table id="z5403f6c4b9f144edab97128b90f28aa5" class="DSPFListTable" style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; WIDTH: 100%" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0">

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif">According to the Economic Policy Institute, average hourly wages for black men are 78 percent of those of similarly situated white men. Wages for black women are 66 percent of those of comparable white men and 88 percent of those received by white women.<sup style="vertical-align: text-top; line-height: 1; font-size: smaller;FONT-SIZE: smaller; VERTICAL-ALIGN: text-top; LINE-HEIGHT: 1">2</sup></div>

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; FONT-WEIGHT: bold; TEXT-ALIGN: left; MARGIN-LEFT: 18pt; TEXT-INDENT: -18pt"><u>2) Pay equity issues are especially important in the retail sector.</u></div>

<div style="TEXT-ALIGN: left; MARGIN-LEFT: 18pt; TEXT-INDENT: -18pt"><br>
</div>

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; TEXT-ALIGN: left; MARGIN-LEFT: 18pt; TEXT-INDENT: -18pt"><font style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">Low pay affects virtually all front-line retail workers.</font> A 2015 report by Demos stated:</div>

<div style="TEXT-ALIGN: left; MARGIN-LEFT: 18pt; TEXT-INDENT: -18pt"><br>
</div>

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; FONT-STYLE: italic; TEXT-ALIGN: left; MARGIN-LEFT: 18pt">Nine percent of all retail workers live below the official poverty line, a higher share than among the workforce as a whole. Another 19 percent live near poverty, with household earnings between 100 and 200 percent of the poverty threshold&#8230; Together, that means more than one in 4 retail workers are poor or living on the edge of hardship.<sup style="vertical-align: text-top; line-height: 1; font-size: smaller;FONT-SIZE: smaller; VERTICAL-ALIGN: text-top; LINE-HEIGHT: 1">3</sup></div>

<div style="TEXT-ALIGN: left; MARGIN-LEFT: 18pt; TEXT-INDENT: -18pt"><br>
</div>

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; TEXT-ALIGN: left">And the notion that retail employees are primarily young, free of dependents, and only temporarily working for such low wages appears to be false.<sup style="vertical-align: text-top; line-height: 1; font-size: smaller;FONT-SIZE: smaller; VERTICAL-ALIGN: text-top; LINE-HEIGHT: 1">4</sup></div>

<div style="TEXT-ALIGN: left; MARGIN-LEFT: 18pt; TEXT-INDENT: -18pt"><br>
</div>

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; FONT-WEIGHT: bold; TEXT-ALIGN: left">Disparate wages, promotion opportunities, and workplace policies make matters worse for people of color employed in the retail sector.</div>

<div><br>
</div>

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; TEXT-ALIGN: left">On average, black and Latino full-time retail salespersons in the U.S. earn just 75 percent of the wages of comparable white workers. As with women, this results in part from the fact that &#8220;people of color are overrepresented in the positions with the lowest pay and the least stability and underrepresented in management positions.&#8221;<sup style="vertical-align: text-top; line-height: 1; font-size: smaller;FONT-SIZE: smaller; VERTICAL-ALIGN: text-top; LINE-HEIGHT: 1">5</sup></div>

<div style="TEXT-ALIGN: left; MARGIN-LEFT: 18pt; TEXT-INDENT: -18pt">&#160;</div>

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; FONT-WEIGHT: bold; TEXT-ALIGN: right; MARGIN-LEFT: 36pt; TEXT-INDENT: -18pt">Page: 3</div>

<div>&#160;</div>

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; TEXT-ALIGN: left">For example, black workers are 11 percent of the U.S. retail workforce, but they hold just 6 percent of management roles &#8212; the retail jobs which typically offer higher pay, full-time hours, and more stable schedules that benefit families. Similarly, Latinos hold just 8 percent of retail management jobs while making up 16 percent of the overall retail workforce. Black and Latino retail workers are also more likely than white workers to work part time when they want full-time hours &#8212; making it harder to contribute to family budgets and more likely that they will be subject to disruptive just-in-time scheduling practices.<sup style="vertical-align: text-top; line-height: 1; font-size: smaller;FONT-SIZE: smaller; VERTICAL-ALIGN: text-top; LINE-HEIGHT: 1">6</sup></div>

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; TEXT-ALIGN: left; MARGIN-LEFT: 18pt; TEXT-INDENT: -18pt">&#160;&#160;</div>

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; FONT-WEIGHT: bold; TEXT-ALIGN: left; MARGIN-LEFT: 18pt; TEXT-INDENT: -18pt"><u>3) WMT&#8217;s reporting on the issue is inadequate.</u></div>

<div style="TEXT-ALIGN: left; MARGIN-LEFT: 18pt; TEXT-INDENT: -18pt"><br>
</div>

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; TEXT-ALIGN: left"><font style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">The current state of WMT&#8217;s disclosure on diversity and inclusion does not address potential pay gaps based on race or ethnicity.</font> In its Statement in Opposition to this proposal, WMT states that their &#8220;compensation plans and programs are designed to encourage high performance and fairly reward our associates regardless of their race, ethnicity, gender, or other personal demographic.&#8221;<sup style="vertical-align: text-top; line-height: 1; font-size: smaller;FONT-SIZE: smaller; VERTICAL-ALIGN: text-top; LINE-HEIGHT: 1">7</sup> However, the Company does not provide data on potential race or ethnicity-based pay gaps in its workforce. Nor does WMT discuss any quantitative goals for reducing gaps as requested in the proposal.</div>

<div style="TEXT-ALIGN: left; MARGIN-LEFT: 18pt; TEXT-INDENT: -18pt"><br>
</div>

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; FONT-WEIGHT: bold; TEXT-ALIGN: left">WMT&#8217;s workforce diversity disclosure lacks detail:</div>

<div><br>
</div>

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; TEXT-ALIGN: left">WMT reports that people of color account for 44 percent of the Company&#8217;s U.S. workforce, but only 32 percent of its U.S. managers.<sup style="vertical-align: text-top; line-height: 1; font-size: smaller;FONT-SIZE: smaller; VERTICAL-ALIGN: text-top; LINE-HEIGHT: 1">8</sup> That disclosure is not detailed enough to determine how typically under-represented groups, such as black people and Latinos, are represented in the overall workforce and, importantly, in management.<sup style="vertical-align: text-top; line-height: 1; font-size: smaller;FONT-SIZE: smaller; VERTICAL-ALIGN: text-top; LINE-HEIGHT: 1">9</sup> WMT does not say which groups are included in its &#8220;people of color&#8221; category. Investors can only speculate about the status of black people and Latinos in management and about accompanying pay gaps that may exist.</div>

<div><br>
</div>

<div style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; TEXT-ALIGN: left">We are concerned that inadequate disclosure may reflect inadequate attention to key risks and opportunities around equity, diversity, and inclusion.</div>
