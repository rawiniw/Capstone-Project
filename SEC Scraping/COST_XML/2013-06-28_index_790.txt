<P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:8%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">The T. Rowe Price Stable Value Fund is a Common Commingled Trust Fund invested primarily in guaranteed investment contracts (GICs). The

comparable investment durations. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:8%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Investment contracts held by a defined-contribution plan are required to be reported at fair

<P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Authoritative guidance defines fair value as the price that would be received to sell an asset or paid to transfer a liability (an exit
price) in an orderly transaction between market participants at the measurement date. This guidance also established a fair value hierarchy that requires maximizing the use of observable inputs when measuring fair value. The three levels of inputs
that may be used are: </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Level&nbsp;1: Quoted market prices in active markets for identical assets or liabilities. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Level&nbsp;2: Observable market-based inputs or unobservable inputs that are corroborated by market data. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Level&nbsp;3: Significant unobservable inputs that are not corroborated by market data. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">The following valuation techniques are used to measure fair value: </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Level&nbsp;1 primarily consists of financial instruments, such as investments in registered investment company funds and Costco Common
Stock, whose value is based on quoted market prices, such as quoted net asset values published by the fund as supported in an active market, exchange-traded instruments and listed equities. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Level&nbsp;2 includes assets and liabilities where quoted market prices are unobservable but observable inputs other than Level&nbsp;1
prices, such as quoted prices for similar assets, or other inputs that are observable or can be corroborated by observable market data for substantially the full term of the assets or liabilities. The Plan&#146;s Level&nbsp;2 assets include
investments in common commingled trust funds. Valuation methodologies are based on &#147;consensus pricing,&#148; using market prices from a variety of industry-standard data providers or pricing that considers various assumptions, including time
value, yield curve, volatility factors, credit spreads, default rates, loss severity, current market and contractual prices for the underlying instruments or debt, broker and dealer quotes, as well as other relevant economic measures. All are
observable in the market or can be derived principally from or corroborated by observable market data, for which the Plan typically receives independent external valuation information. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px; margin-left:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">The Plan had no Level 3 assets or liabilities as of December&nbsp;31, 2012 and 2011. </FONT></P>
