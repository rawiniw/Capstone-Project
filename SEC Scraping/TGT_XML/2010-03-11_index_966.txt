<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">JOHN STUMPF APPOINTED TO TARGET CORPORATION&#146;S</font></b></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">BOARD OF DIRECTORS</font></b></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">George Tamke Retires from Target&#146;s Board</font></b></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Richard Kovacevich to Retire in December</font></b></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">MINNEAPOLIS,
March&nbsp;11, 2010 &#151; Target Corporation (NYSE:TGT) announced today that John
Stumpf, Chairman, President and CEO of Wells Fargo&nbsp;&amp; Company
(NYSE:WFC), has been appointed to its Board of Directors, effective immediately.
Stumpf will replace George Tamke, who retired from the board on March&nbsp;9,
2010.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Stumpf
is a 28-year veteran at Wells Fargo, having joined the former Norwest
Corporation (predecessor of Wells Fargo) in 1982. He was named Chief Executive
Officer in June&nbsp;2007, elected to Wells Fargo&#146;s
Board of Directors in June&nbsp;2006, and has been President since August&nbsp;2005.
He became Chairman at Wells Fargo in January&nbsp;2010.
He serves on the Board of Directors for The Clearing House and the Financial
Services Roundtable. He also serves on the Board of Trustees of the San
Francisco Museum of Modern Art. He earned his bachelor&#146;s degree in finance from
St. Cloud State University, St. Cloud, Minnesota and his MBA with an emphasis
in finance from the University of Minnesota.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;We&#146;re
very pleased to welcome John to our Board,&#148; said Gregg Steinhafel, Chairman,
President and Chief Executive Officer of Target Corporation. &#147;The Board and the
company will benefit from John&#146;s substantial banking experience and business insights,
particularly with respect to our credit card and real estate strategies.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">The
Board has not made a determination regarding any committee assignments for Mr.&nbsp;Stumpf.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">George
Tamke has served on Target&#146;s Board since 1999. He is a partner at Clayton,
Dubilier,&nbsp;&amp; Rice, a private investment firm. He is a director of
Culligan, Ltd., Hertz Global Holdings,&nbsp;Inc. and Service Master Global
Holdings,&nbsp;Inc.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;We
are very grateful for George&#146;s contributions to Target&#146;s Board, and wish him
the best of luck in his future pursuits.&#148; said Steinhafel. During Tamke&#146;s
tenure, Target nearly doubled its store count and made several key strategic
changes, including the decision to sell its Mervyn&#146;s and Marshall Field&#146;s
divisions.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Target
also announced today that Richard Kovacevich, former Chairman and CEO of Wells
Fargo&nbsp;&amp; Company, intends to retire from Target&#146;s Board of Directors on
December&nbsp;31, 2010. &#160;Kovacevich has
served on Target&#146;s Board since 1996.</font></p>
