RESULTS</font></b></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;punctuation-wrap:simple;text-align:center;text-autospace:none;"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:12.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;punctuation-wrap:simple;text-autospace:none;"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:12.0pt;">MINNEAPOLIS,
April&nbsp;8, 2010 -- Target Corporation (NYSE:TGT) today reported that its net
retail sales for the five weeks ended April&nbsp;3, 2010 were $6,233 million,
an increase of 12.5 percent from $5,543 million for the five weeks ended April&nbsp;4,
2009. On this same basis, March&nbsp;comparable-store sales increased 10.3
percent.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;punctuation-wrap:simple;text-autospace:none;"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:12.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;punctuation-wrap:simple;text-autospace:none;"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:12.0pt;">&#147;March&nbsp;comparable-store
sales exceeded our expectations, driven by particular strength in our apparel
business,&#148; said Gregg Steinhafel, chairman, president and chief executive
officer of Target Corporation. &#147;The combination of actual March&nbsp;and
projected April&nbsp;sales performance is expected to produce a 3 to 5 percent
increase in same store sales for this nine-week period.&#160;&#160; Additionally, both of our business segments
are on track to well exceed their respective profit plans for the first
quarter.&#160; As a result, we expect Target&#146;s
first quarter EPS to exceed by 10 cents or more the current First Call median
estimate of 74 cents.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;punctuation-wrap:simple;text-autospace:none;"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:12.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;punctuation-wrap:simple;text-autospace:none;"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:12.0pt;">For
reference, the highest EPS from continuing operations in Target&#146;s history
(excluding holiday-driven fourth quarter results) was 82 cents, achieved in the
second quarter of 2008.&#160; All earnings per
share figures refer to diluted earnings per share.</font></p>
