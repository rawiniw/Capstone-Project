<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Item 5.02.&#160; <u>Departure of Directors or Certain Officers; Election of Directors; Appointment of Certain Officers; Compensatory Arrangements of Certain Officers</u>.</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">On July&nbsp;31, 2014 Target Corporation (&#147;Target&#148;) announced that Brian C. Cornell has been appointed to the position of chief executive officer effective August&nbsp;12, 2014.&#160; Mr.&nbsp;Cornell has also been elected to the Board of Directors of Target and been named chairman of the Board, also effective August&nbsp;12, 2014.&#160; Prior to joining Target, Mr.&nbsp;Cornell, 55, served as chief executive officer of Pepsico Americas Foods, a division of Pepsico,&nbsp;Inc., from March&nbsp;2012 until July&nbsp;2014.&#160;&#160; From April&nbsp;2009 to January&nbsp;2012 Mr.&nbsp;Cornell served as chief executive officer and president of Sam&#146;s Club, a division of Wal-Mart Stores,&nbsp;Inc., and as an executive vice president of Wal-Mart Stores,&nbsp;Inc. &#160;A copy of Target&#146;s press release announcing this event, which contains additional information on Mr.&nbsp;Cornell&#146;s background, is attached as Exhibit&nbsp;99 and incorporated herein by reference.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">In connection with this appointment, Mr.&nbsp;Cornell will receive an annual base salary of $1,300,000, will be eligible for a pro-rated annual cash incentive under Target&#146;s Officer Short-Term Incentive Plan for fiscal 2014 with a target incentive opportunity of 150% of base salary, and will be granted stock-based awards under Target&#146;s 2011 Long-Term Incentive Plan having a target payout value of $3,750,000.&#160; The stock-based awards will consist of performance share units (75% of grant value) and performance based restricted stock units (25% of grant value), on terms consistent with the awards granted to Target&#146;s other executive officers in January&nbsp;2014.&#160; The Board has also agreed to grant stock-based awards to Mr.&nbsp;Cornell for fiscal 2015, at the time annual grants are made to executive officers generally, with a target value of $9,000,000, such awards to be on the same terms and in the same form as are made to other executive officers.&#160; Mr.&nbsp;Cornell will be an &#147;at-will&#148; employee of Target, and will have no specified term as chief executive officer.&#160; He will be eligible for benefits under Target&#146;s Officer Income Continuance Policy and other benefits available to executive officers generally.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">To compensate Mr.&nbsp;Cornell for incentive awards from his former employer that he will be forfeiting, the Board has agreed to provide Mr.&nbsp;Cornell with a &#147;Make-Whole Equity Grant&#148; and a &#147;Make-Whole Pro-Rata Annual Bonus Payment.&#148;&#160; The Make-Whole Equity Grant will have a value of $19,250,000 <u>less</u> the target value of any incentive awards from his former employer that he is eligible to retain (which has not been determined as of the date of this filing).&#160; The Make-Whole Equity Grant will consist of two components:&#160; (i)&nbsp;30% will be in the form of restricted stock units that will vest in March&nbsp;2015; and (ii)&nbsp;70% will be in the form of performance based restricted stock units that will vest in 1/3 increments in March&nbsp;2016, 2017 and 2018, respectively, subject to his continued employment through the applicable vesting dates.&#160; Performance based restricted stock units have a variable payout ranging from 75% to 125% of the target value depending on whether Target&#146;s total return to shareholders is in the bottom, middle or top third of its retail peer group.&#160; If Mr.&nbsp;Cornell&#146;s employment is involuntarily terminated without cause before an applicable vesting date, Mr.&nbsp;Cornell will receive 100% of the unvested restricted stock units, and 50% of the unvested performance based restricted stock units.&#160; The Make-Whole Pro-Rata Annual Bonus Payment represents the value of Mr.&nbsp;Cornell&#146;s target annual cash incentive from his former employer ($1,350,000), and will be pro-rated to reflect the number of days since the start of his former employer&#146;s fiscal year to the effective date of his appointment as Target&#146;s chief executive officer.&#160; The Make-Whole Pro-Rata Annual Bonus Payment will be paid by Target at the time payments, if any, are made under Target&#146;s Officer Short-Term Incentive Plan for fiscal 2014, subject to Mr.&nbsp;Cornell&#146;s continued employment with Target through such date.</font></p>

Cornell, 55, joins Target with more than 30 years of experience at some of the nation&#146;s leading retail and consumer product companies. As Target&#146;s new CEO, Cornell&#146;s top priorities will be accelerating the company&#146;s performance and advancing Target&#146;s omnichannel evolution.</font></p>

He was responsible for a portfolio that included Frito-Lay North America, Quaker Foods and all of PepsiCo&#146;s Latin America food and snack businesses. Before joining PepsiCo in 2012, Cornell served as president and CEO of Sam&#146;s Club, a division of Wal-Mart Stores,&nbsp;Inc. Cornell also held the position of CEO at Michaels Stores,&nbsp;Inc., and prior to that, executive vice president and chief marketing officer for Safeway.</font></p>

Austin, interim non-executive chair of the board. &#147;The Board is confident that Brian&#146;s diverse and broad experience in retail and consumer products as well as his passion for leading high performing teams will propel Target forward.&#148;</font></p>
