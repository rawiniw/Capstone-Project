<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Item 5.02.</font></b><b><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;font-weight:bold;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font></b><b><u><font size="2" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Departure of Directors or Certain Officers; Election of Directors; Appointment of Certain Officers; Compensatory Arrangements of Certain Officers</font></u></b><b><font size="2" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">.</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">This Form&nbsp;8-K/A amends the Form&nbsp;8-K filed on May&nbsp;5, 2014, in which Target Corporation announced that Gregg W. Steinhafel had stepped down as President and Chief Executive Officer, and resigned as a Director, effective May&nbsp;5, 2014.&#160; On May&nbsp;14, 2014, the Board approved the compensation-related aspects of Mr.&nbsp;Steinhafel&#146;s departure and transition.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">As disclosed previously, in connection with Mr.&nbsp;Steinhafel&#146;s departure from Target, he is eligible to receive severance benefits under Target&#146;s Income Continuance Policy (&#147;ICP&#148;).&#160; In accordance with the ICP, as a condition to severance payment eligibility, Mr.&nbsp;Steinhafel must sign an agreement that includes a non-solicitation clause and a release of claims, and provides that severance payments may be recovered and that any outstanding equity awards held by him may be terminated if he becomes employed by specified competitors.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Mr.&nbsp;Steinhafel has agreed to remain employed by Target in an advisory capacity to assist with the transition through no later than August&nbsp;23, 2014.&#160; During this advisory period, he will continue to receive the same base salary and benefits that were in effect on the date he stepped down as President and CEO.&#160; In addition, due to Mr.&nbsp;Steinhafel&#146;s age and years of service with Target, under the pre-existing program he will remain eligible for a fiscal 2014 short-term incentive opportunity under Target&#146;s Short-Term Incentive Plan based on Target&#146;s actual financial performance, with the cash payout, if any, pro-rated based on his length of employment during the year. The Board determined that the amount of the short-term incentive payout opportunity for the portion of the payout, if any, attributable to the advisory period will be based on the same terms as in effect on the date he stepped down as President and CEO. After the advisory period ends, Mr.&nbsp;Steinhafel will begin receiving the severance benefits described above. All other compensation and benefits provided to Mr.&nbsp;Steinhafel are consistent with Target&#146;s pre-existing compensation plans.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Also on May&nbsp;14, 2014, the Board increased John J. Mulligan&#146;s base salary from $700,000 to $1 million, and approved a one-time grant, effective May&nbsp;22, 2014, of restricted stock units for Mr.&nbsp;Mulligan in the amount of $1 million that will vest in one-third increments on each anniversary of the grant date. The restricted stock units have the same general terms as the restricted stock units that have been granted to executive officers from time to time, except that the units will vest in full in the event that Mr.&nbsp;Mulligan&#146;s employment is involuntarily terminated without cause.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">In connection with the appointment of Mr.&nbsp;Mulligan to the additional capacities of Interim President and Chief Executive Officer, the Board increased his fiscal 2014 short-term incentive opportunity from 80% to 90% of his base salary. The increase in the </font><font size="2" style="font-size:10.0pt;">short-term incentive opportunity will be pro-rated for the time period during which Mr.&nbsp;Mulligan serves in the additional capacities.</font></p>
